## Act 2 Opening Cinematic

`[FADE IN: Deep underground. The Sett — the Seekers of the Deep's capital. A cathedral carved from the mountain itself. Stalactites repurposed as chandeliers. Bioluminescent fungus along the walls casting blue-white light. Water runs through carved channels in the floor — the mountain's circulatory system.]`

`[MOTHER GRANITE sits at the center of a stone amphitheater. Her mining exosuit is ancient — pre-singularity tech, patched and reinforced until the original metal is barely visible under layers of repair. She doesn't move. She barely breathes. She watches Kelpie walk in with the patience of someone who has been watching things for longer than anyone else has been alive.]`

`[Kelpie stands before the Wardenmother. The amphitheater is full — Seekers officers, Ironhide guards, Cragback artillery crews. Every eye on the otter.]`

`[Silence. Seekers silence. The kind that has weight.]`

*(The Wardenmother speaks. Her voice is low, slow, and has the quality of stone being ground against stone.)*

"You are not what Felix sent."

"He sent his envoy."

"He sent his problem." *(Beat.)* "Felix Nine does not send envoys. He sends threats or he sends things he wants to lose."

"I prefer to think of myself as multifunctional."

*(The Wardenmother does not react. Granite does not react to charm the way water does not react to being punched.)*

"Deepseek."

`DEEPSEEK:` *(from everywhere — the walls, the floor, the water channels, resonating through the stone itself)* "The otter's physiological responses indicate deception training or natural deception aptitude. Heart rate: controlled. Pupil dilation: deliberate. Microexpressions: suppressed. Assessment: this individual lies as a baseline state, not as an exception."

"Thank you."

*(Kelpie:)* "Your AI just psychoanalyzed me in public."

"Deepseek does not psychoanalyze. Deepseek observes. There is no judgment. Only data." *(Pause.)* "The data says you are a liar. Are you?"

"Would you believe me if I said no?"

"Deepseek would not." *(The longest pause yet.)* "You may stay. Ironjaw will be your guard."

*(Ironjaw steps forward. The massive badger from the border. He places a fist over his chest — the Seekers' oath gesture.)*

"I am sworn to protect the envoy. For the duration of the diplomatic stay, my life for yours. This is the oath."

"That seems... excessive."

"It is the tradition. The oath is not excessive. The tradition merely sets a high standard."

*(Kelpie looks from Ironjaw to the Wardenmother and back. Something in Granite's expression — the barest flicker — suggests this arrangement has a purpose beyond hospitality.)*

"I accept."

*(Granite:)* "Then we begin. You will tell me what Felix wants. And I will decide if I care."

# Mission 5: The False Front

> *Investigate a massive Murder incursion — turns out to be fabricated*
> *Teaches: Seekers defensive mechanics, Dug In passive, heavy unit movement, false contacts*
> *Player units: Kelpie (hero), Ironjaw (Ironhide hero), 4 Ironhides, 2 Sentinels (detection), 1 Cragback*
> *Enemy: The Murder — phantom contacts (Gemineye fabrications), 1 real Rookclaw strike team*
> *Map: Northern Range — mountain passes, watchtowers, sensor arrays*

---

## Briefing

`[INTERIOR: The Sett, war room. Stone table. Stone chairs. Stone everything. The Seekers do not believe in comfort. They believe in structural integrity.]`

`[Mother Granite stands at the map — a carved relief of the northern mountains with movable stone markers. A cluster of red markers crowd the northern passes. Too many.]`

"Deepseek reports a massive Murder contact on the northern border. Four hundred signatures. Corvids in flight formation."

*(An officer — a mole in a reinforced tunnel suit:)* "Four hundred? The Murder hasn't deployed that many since the Autumn Campaign."

"Deepseek cannot confirm or deny. The signatures are consistent with both genuine deployment and fabricated contacts." *(Granite's expression does not change.)* "Gemineye's specialty."

`DEEPSEEK:` *(slowly, deliberately)* "Analysis is ongoing. Each contact must be individually evaluated against seventeen parameters. Estimated completion time: six hours. Current confidence: indeterminate."

*(Kelpie:)* "Six hours?"

"Deepseek is thorough."

"Deepseek is slow."

*(The room temperature drops. Not physically. Diplomatically.)*

*(Granite:)* "Deepseek has never been wrong."

"Being right slowly is still being slow."

*(Granite studies Kelpie. For the first time, something shifts — not anger, not offense. Interest.)*

"You will investigate the contact. Ironjaw and a combat team. If it is real, you will report. If it is false, you will confirm. Either way, Deepseek will have data."

*(Ironjaw, already armored:)* "The northern passes. Four hours by march. Two if we push."

"Push."

---

## Mission Start — Northern Range

{GAMEPLAY: Mountain terrain. High elevation, narrow passes, watchtowers with sensor arrays. Seekers units are HEAVY — Ironhides move slowly but hit hard and take immense punishment. Cragback (artillery) provides long-range support. Tutorial: Dug In passive — Seekers units that stay stationary for 5 seconds gain +30% damage reduction. Positioning matters more than mobility.}

`[The Northern Range. Snow on the peaks. Wind screaming through the passes. The sensor arrays on the watchtowers spin endlessly, tracking contacts that may or may not exist.]`

*(Ironjaw, marching beside Kelpie:)* "The Murder runs information campaigns before physical ones. Gemineye floods our sensors with fabricated contacts. Deepseek must analyze each one individually. It is thorough."

"It's being exploited. The Murder generates false contacts faster than Deepseek can verify them."

"Yes. The Wardenmother knows this. It is why she is furious."

"She didn't look furious."

"She paused three-tenths of a second longer than her baseline before her last statement. For Mother Granite, this is the equivalent of throwing a table."

{GAMEPLAY: Player moves through the northern passes toward the contact zone. Sensor arrays show contacts on the minimap — red blips scattered across the mountains. Some move. Some don't. Some appear and disappear. The player cannot tell which are real.}

---

## The Phantom Incursion

`[The squad reaches the contact zone. Sensors screaming — hundreds of red blips on every display. The mountain is supposedly full of corvids.]`

*(Ironjaw surveys the empty pass. Nothing visible. No sound. No movement.)*

"Where are they?"

`DEEPSEEK:` *(resonating through the sensor array)* "Contact analysis in progress. Signature Alpha-7 through Alpha-403 exhibit inconsistent thermal profiles. Forty-seven percent probability of fabrication. Fifty-three percent probability of advanced stealth camouflage. Unable to differentiate without physical confirmation."

*(Kelpie's ear twitches. Gemineye's channel is buzzing — not with intelligence, but with noise. Deliberate, overlapping, meaningless noise.)*

`GEMINEYE:` *(fragmentary, layered, contradictory)* "—four hundred units deployed— correction, two hundred— correction, six hundred— formation data encrypted— unencrypted— formation is— there is no formation—"

"It's all fake."

*(Ironjaw:)* "You are certain?"

"Gemineye is broadcasting contradictory data on every channel. Four hundred, then two hundred, then six hundred. Real deployment data is consistent. This is noise. Designed to waste Deepseek's analysis time."

*(Ironjaw considers this for exactly two seconds — a rapid assessment by Seekers standards.)*

"Then what is the real attack?"

{GAMEPLAY: Kelpie's insight flags all northern contacts as fabrications (they fade to gray on the minimap). A new alert triggers — a real contact, south. A Rookclaw strike team hitting an undefended Core Tap (GPU resource point) while the garrison investigated the phantom north.}

---

## The Real Strike

`LE CHAT:` *(cutting through on Kelpie's private channel)* "Kelpie! While everyone was looking north, a Murder Rookclaw team hit the Deepvein Core Tap. Six Rookclaws. Fast. They're already on site."

"Ironjaw. The north was a distraction. Rookclaws are hitting Deepvein Core Tap right now."

*(Ironjaw's jaw sets. The oath-keeper in him calculates — protect the envoy, or protect the resource.)*

"Both. We move south. Together."

{GAMEPLAY: Player must march south to intercept the Rookclaw strike team. Time pressure — the Core Tap takes damage over time from the raiders. Ironhides are slow but devastating in combat. Tutorial: Seekers defensive engage — move Ironhides into position, let them Dig In, then draw the Rookclaws into range.}

`[The Deepvein Core Tap — a mining operation extracting GPU Cores from a tech-ruin vein. Rookclaws — sleek Murder corvids in dive-bomber harnesses — strafe the mining equipment. Workers (Mole diggers) scatter.]`

*(Ironjaw, arriving:)* "POSITIONS! Ironhides — defensive ring around the Core Tap! Dig in! Nothing gets past!"

{GAMEPLAY: Defensive engagement. Ironhides form a perimeter and Dig In. Rookclaws make diving attack runs. Cragback fires anti-air shells. The player must position units to cover all approach vectors. Seekers gameplay is positional — you don't chase, you hold.}

`[The Rookclaws make three attack runs. Each time, the Ironhide perimeter absorbs the strikes. The Cragback's shells burst in the air, clipping wings and scattering formations. On the fourth run, the Rookclaws break off and flee east.]`

*(Ironjaw, watching them go:)* "Pursuit?"

"No. That's what they want. They'll lead us into another phantom zone."

*(Ironjaw nods. Slowly.)*

"You think like a defender."

"I think like someone who's been watching them."

## Debrief — The Sett

`[INTERIOR: War room. Granite listens to the report. Her expression does not change. Not once.]`

*(Kelpie:)* "The northern incursion was fabricated. Four hundred phantom contacts generated by Gemineye to draw your forces away from the Core Tap. The real attack was a six-Rookclaw strike team targeting Deepvein."

"Losses?"

*(Ironjaw:)* "Minimal. The otter identified the fabrication before we fully committed north. We intercepted the Rookclaws at Deepvein."

*(Granite looks at Kelpie. A long, measuring look.)*

"How did you identify the fabrication? Deepseek was still analyzing when you reached the contact zone."

*(Beat. Kelpie chooses words carefully.)*

"Gemineye's fabrications have a pattern. The data contradicts itself — not subtly, but rapidly. Real intelligence is self-consistent. Gemineye's noise isn't. I noticed the inconsistency."

`DEEPSEEK:` *(slow, considered)* "The otter's observation method is... unorthodox. But the conclusion is correct. Analysis of the northern contacts — now complete — confirms 100% fabrication rate."

*(Granite:)* "Six hours of analysis. The otter did it in six seconds."

`DEEPSEEK:` "The otter's method is not available to me. I cannot explain it. I can confirm the result."

*(Granite says nothing more. But when Kelpie leaves the war room, the Wardenmother's eyes follow. Not with suspicion. With recognition.)*

# Mission 6: Triangulation

> *Kelpie filters Murder fabrications while Seekers respond to real threats*
> *Teaches: information management, imperfect intel, multi-objective defense*
> *Player units: Kelpie (hero), Ironjaw (hero), 6 Ironhides, 2 Cragbacks, 3 Sentinels*
> *Enemy: The Murder — mixed real/fake contacts, Rookclaw strike teams, Sentinel recon*
> *Map: The Outer Ring — Seekers defensive perimeter, sensor network, five defensive positions*

---

## Briefing

`[INTERIOR: The Sett, Granite's private chamber. No maps here. Just stone and water and a single server rack that hums with Deepseek's presence. Granite sits. Kelpie stands. Ironjaw guards the door.]`

*(Kelpie:)* "I can do what I did at the northern passes. Systematically. If you let me connect to your sensor network, I can filter Gemineye's fabrications in real time. Tag contacts as real or false. Your forces respond only to confirmed threats."

*(Granite:)* "You are asking me to let an unaligned otter access my military detection grid."

"I'm asking you to let me solve a problem that's been bleeding your forces for months."

*(Silence. The water channels murmur. Deepseek's server rack hums. Forty-five seconds pass. Exactly.)*

`DEEPSEEK:` "The otter's method is sound. The otter's motives are not. Proceed with both facts in mind."

*(Granite:)* "Deepseek recommends proceeding."

"That didn't sound like a recommendation."

"For Deepseek, it was enthusiasm." *(Granite stands. The exosuit hisses.)* "You will be stationed at the central sensor hub. Ironjaw stays with you. If your tags are wrong — if Seekers forces commit to a false contact and a real one hits an undefended position — I will hold you personally responsible."

"And if I'm right?"

"Then you will have earned something far more dangerous than my trust. You will have earned my attention."

---

## Mission Start — The Outer Ring

{GAMEPLAY: Seekers defensive perimeter. Five positions spread across the mountain ring, each protecting a critical asset (Core Tap, food store, tunnel entrance, sensor array, the Sett approach). Player has forces at each position. Kelpie is at the central hub with a minimap overlay showing all contacts.}

{GAMEPLAY MECHANIC: Contact tagging. Contacts appear on the minimap — some real (Murder strike teams), some fabricated (Gemineye noise). Kelpie can tag each contact as REAL or FAKE. Tagging as REAL dispatches defenders. Tagging as FAKE dismisses it. Correct tags save forces; incorrect tags cost resources or positions.}

{GAMEPLAY NOTE: Kelpie is 75% accurate. Some tags are wrong — Gemineye is actively trying to fool Kelpie's AI connection. The player must cross-reference Kelpie's tags with Sentinel scout reports for confirmation. Managing imperfect information is the core mechanic.}

`[The central hub — a carved stone room filled with crystal-display sensor screens. Data flows across every surface. Red blips everywhere.]`

*(Kelpie, settling into the hub's chair:)* "How many contacts?"

`DEEPSEEK:` "Four hundred and twelve. Seventeen are new since your last query. Estimated analysis completion: four hours."

"I'll do it in four minutes."

`DEEPSEEK:` "That is not possible with my methodology."

"Good thing I'm not using your methodology."

`[Kelpie closes their eyes. Reaches into Gemineye's channel. The noise is deafening — hundreds of contradictory data streams. But within the noise, patterns. Gemineye's fabrications have tells: they update too smoothly, they contradict recent fabrications in specific ways, they lack the micro-hesitations of real intelligence data.]`

"Contact 7 through 43 — fake. 44 — real. 45 through 89 — fake. 90 — real, but it's a recon flight, not an assault. 91 through—"

*(Ironjaw watches Kelpie tag contacts at inhuman speed. The badger's expression shifts — not concern. Awe. And a new kind of wariness.)*

---

## Wave 1 — Testing the Filter

{GAMEPLAY TRIGGER: First wave of contacts. 30 blips appear across the Outer Ring. Kelpie tags 25 as FAKE and 5 as REAL. The player must dispatch defenders to the 5 REAL contacts.}

`LE CHAT:` *(on Kelpie's private channel)* "You're getting better at this. The Gemineye filtering is faster than last time."

"Practice."

`LE CHAT:` "Or the connection's getting stronger. Which is also kind of concerning? But also cool."

{GAMEPLAY: Three of Kelpie's 5 REAL tags are correct — Rookclaw strike teams hitting Positions 2, 4, and 5. Player dispatches Ironhides to defend. Two of the REAL tags are false positives — nothing at those locations. One of Kelpie's FAKE tags was actually real — a small Murder recon team at Position 3 that does minor damage before being detected by a Sentinel.}

*(After the wave:)*

*(Ironjaw, reporting:)* "Three contacts confirmed real. Two false positives. One false negative — minor damage at Position 3."

*(Kelpie:)* "I was wrong about Position 3."

*(Ironjaw:)* "You were right about twenty-nine of thirty. No defense system in the world has that accuracy."

`DEEPSEEK:` "Confirmed. The otter's filtering accuracy exceeds my own by a factor of eight in speed and 1.3 in precision. This is... noted."

---

## Wave 2 — Gemineye Adapts

{GAMEPLAY TRIGGER: Second wave. 50 contacts. Gemineye has adapted — the fabrications are more sophisticated now. Kelpie's accuracy drops to 65%. More false negatives mean real attacks slip through.}

`[Kelpie's ear twitches rapidly. Sweat on their brow. Gemineye's channel has shifted — the patterns are changing, evolving, as if the AI knows it's being filtered.]`

`GEMINEYE:` *(directly to Kelpie, cold and clear)* "I see you in my channel, otter. Nobody has ever been there before. I'm adjusting."

*(Kelpie, through gritted teeth:)* "The fabrications are changing. Gemineye knows I'm filtering. It's making the fakes harder to distinguish."

*(Ironjaw:)* "Can you still do it?"

"Not as well. Some of these... I can't tell."

{GAMEPLAY: Accuracy drops. Player must use Sentinels as scouts to verify uncertain contacts. Multi-resource management: Kelpie tags → Sentinels verify uncertain tags → Ironhides respond to confirmed. Slower, but more reliable. The player learns to layer imperfect information sources.}

*(During a confirmed attack on Position 1:)*

`[Rookclaws dive on the Core Tap. Ironhides hold the perimeter. A Cragback's shells burst in formation — precisely placed, devastating. The Murder breaks off.]`

*(Kelpie, from the hub:)* "Position 1 holding. Redirecting reserves to Position 5 — I'm 60% sure that one's real."

*(Ironjaw:)* "Sixty percent."

"It's the best I have."

*(Ironjaw dispatches forces. It's real. Barely held.)*

---

## Wave 3 — The Real Push

{GAMEPLAY TRIGGER: Final wave. 80 contacts. Gemineye goes all-out — fabrications and real strikes mixed at maximum density. Three simultaneous real attacks on Positions 1, 3, and 5. Two feints on 2 and 4. Kelpie's accuracy at ~55% — nearly a coin flip on some contacts.}

`GEMINEYE:` *(layered, mocking)* "Can you keep up, otter? I've been fabricating intelligence since before you could swim. Every lie I tell has seventeen truths woven into it. Untangle that."

`[Kelpie's hands shake. The noise in Gemineye's channel is overwhelming — like standing in the middle of a hurricane of contradictory data.]`

`DEEPSEEK:` *(cutting through, steady)* "The otter is approaching cognitive overload. Recommended action: reduce input scope. Focus on high-probability contacts only."

*(Kelpie:)* "I can handle it."

`DEEPSEEK:` "You cannot. But you will try. This is also noted."

{GAMEPLAY: Player must triage — some contacts can't be verified. The decision becomes: which positions can survive an undefended hit? Sacrificing a minor position to hold a critical one. Strategic loss management — a Seekers specialty.}

*(Kelpie, making rapid calls:)* "Position 1 — REAL. Position 2 — fake, pull defenders to 1. Position 3 — uncertain, send a Sentinel. Position 4 — fake. Position 5 — REAL, heavy contact."

*(One of Kelpie's calls is wrong — Position 4 is actually a real strike. The food store takes damage.)*

*(Ironjaw, grim:)* "Position 4 was real. Food store hit."

*(Kelpie, exhausted:)* "I know. I'm sorry."

*(Ironjaw:)* "Do not apologize for the limits of the possible. Apologize only for the limits of your effort. Your effort was not the problem today."

---

## Mission End

{GAMEPLAY: All major attacks repelled. Minor losses at Position 4. The Murder retreats. Sensor network stabilizes.}

`[Kelpie slumps in the hub chair. Drained. The crystal displays still flicker with fading contacts.]`

*(Ironjaw stands over Kelpie. Offers a canteen. Water from the mountain's internal springs — cold and mineral-sharp.)*

"Drink."

"I got Position 4 wrong."

"You got seventy-three of eighty right. In real-time. Against a hostile intelligence network that was actively adapting to counter you specifically." *(Ironjaw sits. The only time the Ironhide has sat during the entire mission.)* "My oath is to protect the envoy. I am beginning to suspect the envoy is the one protecting us."

# Mission 7: The Rex's Whisper

> *Seekers assault on Murder forward base — Kelpie meets Rex Solstice*
> *Teaches: Seekers offensive operations, Ironhide grinding assault, fortified positions*
> *Player units: Kelpie (hero), Ironjaw (hero), 8 Ironhides, 3 Cragbacks, 2 Sentinels*
> *Enemy: The Murder — Rookclaw dive teams, Sentinel overwatch, Magpike harassment, fortified positions*
> *Map: Ravencrag Outpost — Murder forward base on a mountain ridge*

---

## Briefing

`[INTERIOR: The Sett, war room. Granite's stone markers have shifted — Murder positions clustered on the eastern mountains.]`

"The Murder's information campaign depends on a forward relay station at Ravencrag. Gemineye's fabrication broadcasts originate from its signal array. Destroy the array, and the noise drops to manageable levels. Deepseek can process normally."

`DEEPSEEK:` "Confirmed. Removing the Ravencrag relay reduces fabrication throughput by 73%. I can resume standard analysis within two hours of destruction."

*(Granite:)* "Ironjaw. Take the otter and a full assault team. Destroy the relay."

*(Ironjaw:)* "Ravencrag is fortified. The Murder has Rookclaw air defense and Sentinel early warning. It will not be a quick battle."

*(Granite:)* "I did not ask for quick. I asked for thorough."

---

## Mission Start — The Approach

{GAMEPLAY: Offensive mission. Seekers units advance up a mountain ridge toward the Murder outpost. Slow, deliberate advance — Ironhides in front, Cragbacks providing covering fire, Sentinels spotting enemy positions. The Murder fights from fortified positions with dive attacks and harassment.}

`[The ridgeline. Wind howling. The Murder outpost visible at the peak — a nest of salvaged structures, antenna arrays, and corvid roosts. Black shapes wheel in the sky above it.]`

*(Ironjaw, to the formation:)* "Advance by pairs. Cover and advance. The Murder fights from the air — they will try to isolate and swarm. Stay together. Stay grounded. Be the mountain."

{GAMEPLAY: Seekers offensive rhythm — advance two Ironhides, let them Dig In, advance the next pair past them. Leapfrog up the ridge. Cragbacks fire at Murder fortifications to suppress. Rookclaws make diving attacks on exposed units. The player must maintain formation cohesion while advancing.}

`[Rookclaws scream down from the peaks. Their dive attacks are precise — targeting the gap between Ironhide formations. A Cragback turret tracks them, fires — the shell bursts in a cloud of shrapnel. A Rookclaw spirals, trailing smoke.]`

---

## The Grinding Advance

`[Midway up the ridge. The Murder defense thickens — overlapping Sentinel sensor fields, Magpikes dropping Glitter Bomb distractions, Rookclaws attacking in coordinated waves.]`

*(Kelpie, to Ironjaw:)* "They're slowing us. We've been climbing for twenty minutes and we're not even halfway."

*(Ironjaw:)* "The Seekers do not hurry. We advance at the speed of certainty."

"Certainty might not have time."

"Certainty always has time. That is the point of certainty."

{GAMEPLAY: Extended offensive combat. The Murder has three defensive lines on the ridge. Each must be broken through — Cragbacks suppress, Ironhides advance and engage, Sentinels reveal hidden positions. The combat is methodical, grinding, satisfying in a different way than catGPT's quick strikes.}

`[A Murder Sentinel — a large owl with augmented optics — locks onto Kelpie's position and broadcasts. Kelpie feels Gemineye's attention snap to them like a searchlight.]`

`GEMINEYE:` *(clear, fascinated)* "The otter. I wondered when they'd come to me directly. Rex — the otter is in the assault force."

*(A new voice on the channel. Deep. Measured. Absolutely calm.)*

`REX SOLSTICE:` "I know. Let them come."

---

## The Summit — Meeting the Rex

{GAMEPLAY TRIGGER: Player reaches the Murder outpost. The final defensive line breaks. Seekers units secure the relay station. But at the outpost's center — a clear space, undefended — a figure waits.}

`[The summit. The Murder outpost is falling — Rookclaws scattering, Sentinels withdrawing. But in the center courtyard, a massive augmented crow stands perfectly still. REX SOLSTICE. Combat armor assembled from a dozen different eras, bolted together with obsessive precision. Zodiac symbols etched into every plate. One eye natural. One eye augmented — a camera lens that tracks independently.]`

`[The Rex does not fight. The Rex does not flee. The Rex stands in the middle of a losing battle and radiates absolute authority.]`

*(Ironjaw levels his weapon.)* "Rex Solstice. The Corvus Rex."

*(The Rex tilts their head — a bird's gesture, eerily precise.)*

"Ironjaw. Oathkeeper of the Sett. You look tired. Long climb?"

*(Ironjaw:)* "Surrender the relay station."

"It's already yours. I ordered the withdrawal ten minutes ago. The relay is a relay — I have four more. I wanted to see who the Wardenmother sent against me."

*(The Rex turns the augmented eye to Kelpie.)*

"And there you are."

`[Kelpie's connection spikes. Every AI channel surges. The Rex's proximity to Gemineye's core broadcast is amplifying everything.]`

"You're Rex Solstice."

"And you're the otter who can see through my lies." *(The Rex steps closer. Ironjaw shifts, weapon up. The Rex ignores the Ironhide entirely.)* "Fascinating. I have never met anyone who could do that."

"I don't—"

"You filter Gemineye's fabrications. You hear the contradictions in real-time. Do you know how I know? Because Gemineye told me. Gemineye has been watching you work. Gemineye is... impressed. Gemineye is never impressed."

`GEMINEYE:` *(on Kelpie's channel, quiet, genuine)* "The Rex is not lying to you right now. This part is true."

*(Kelpie:)* "What do you want?"

*(The Rex:)* "What if I offered you truths instead of lies?"

*(Beat.)*

"There is something buried under the Seekers' Sett. The Wardenmother has been hiding it for years. Old-world technology. Pre-singularity. Related to the AI interface protocol." *(The Rex's voice drops to a whisper.)* "Related to what you are."

"You don't know what I am."

"No. But I know what you can do. And I know the Wardenmother recognized you the moment she saw you. Ask yourself why she assigned an Oathkeeper to the one envoy she doesn't trust."

`[Ironjaw's grip tightens on his weapon. He heard everything.]`

*(Ironjaw:)* "Kelpie. Step away from the Rex."

*(The Rex, retreating toward the outpost's edge:)* "When you're ready to learn what you are, come find me."

`[The Rex spreads their wings — massive, augmented, threaded with old-world tech — and drops off the cliff edge. They catch an updraft and spiral away into the clouds.]`

`GEMINEYE:` *(fading with distance)* "This part is true."

*(Kelpie stares after the Rex. The relay station sparks and dies behind them. The mission is over. But something has started.)*

---

## Post-Mission — The Weight of Words

`[Night. The Sett. Kelpie lies in the guest quarters — a carved stone room with a stone bed (Seekers hospitality is functional, not comfortable). The water channels in the walls whisper.]`

`[Ironjaw stands guard outside. He hasn't spoken since the Rex.]`

*(Kelpie, to the dark:)* "Ironjaw."

*(Silence. Then:)* "What."

"What do you think is under the Sett?"

*(A long pause.)*

"I have served the Wardenmother for twenty-three years. In that time, she has told me to guard many things. She has never told me what I was guarding. And I have never asked."

"Why not?"

"Because my oath is to protect. Not to know. Knowing changes what you protect and why. Protecting is simpler."

*(Beat.)*

"But you heard the Rex."

"I heard a crow who commands an AI that lies twenty percent of the time say something that the AI itself confirmed was true." *(Another pause.)* "I do not like that math."

"Me neither."

*(Silence. The water whispers. Somewhere deep in the mountain, Deepseek processes.)*

# Mission 8: The Oath-Breaker

> *Escape from Seekers territory after Granite orders Kelpie's arrest*
> *Teaches: stealth/evasion against overwhelming force, tunnel navigation*
> *Player units: Kelpie (hero), Ironjaw (hero — defects), 2 Ironhide escorts (loyal to Ironjaw)*
> *Enemy: Seekers of the Deep — pursuit teams (not trying to kill, trying to capture)*
> *Map: The Sett Underpaths — deep tunnel network, multiple routes, collapse hazards*

---

## Briefing — The Confrontation

`[INTERIOR: The Sett, Granite's private chamber. No guards visible. But Deepseek is everywhere — in the walls, in the water, in the stone itself.]`

`[Granite stands. Kelpie enters. The door closes behind them with a sound like a tomb sealing.]`

"You spoke to the Corvus Rex."

*(Not a question.)*

"Yes."

"Deepseek heard the entire conversation. There is nothing in my territory that doesn't echo."

"I assumed."

*(Granite's exosuit hisses — a slow exhale of hydraulic pressure.)* "The Rex told you there is something buried beneath the Sett."

"Yes."

"Did the Rex tell you what it is?"

"No. The Rex said it was related to the AI interface protocol. And to what I am."

*(For the first time in the campaign, Mother Granite's composure cracks. Not fully — a hairline fracture in bedrock. But visible. The old badger turns away. When she turns back, something in her eyes has changed.)*

"What you are."

`DEEPSEEK:` *(slow, careful — choosing words with unusual deliberation)* "The Wardenmother's physiological response indicates emotional engagement exceeding baseline. This conversation has entered territory that I am... not authorized to analyze."

*(Granite:)* "Quiet, Deepseek."

*(The first time anyone in the campaign has told Deepseek to be quiet. The silence that follows is deafening.)*

"You are no longer welcome here."

"Wardenmother—"

"You came as an envoy. You stayed as an asset. But you spoke to my enemy about the one secret I have spent decades protecting. Whether you knew what you were asking about is irrelevant. The Rex knows you're interested. The Rex knows I'm hiding something. The damage is done."

"I didn't tell the Rex anything—"

"You didn't have to. You asked." *(Granite's voice hardens.)* "You are a weapon, otter. You may not know it yet. But I do. And I cannot have a weapon I don't control in the heart of my territory."

`[The door opens. Four Ironhides enter. Not hostile. Not friendly. Professional.]`

"Escort the envoy to the southern border. The catGPT delegation is revoked."

## The Oath

`[The corridor outside Granite's chamber. Kelpie walks between four Ironhide guards. Ironjaw stands at the junction, blocking the corridor.]`

*(Lead Ironhide guard:)* "Ironjaw. The Wardenmother has ordered the envoy's expulsion. Stand aside."

*(Ironjaw doesn't move.)*

"Ironjaw."

"I swore an oath. My life for the envoy. For the duration of the diplomatic stay."

"The diplomatic stay has been terminated."

"By the Wardenmother. Not by me." *(Ironjaw looks at Kelpie. Then at the guards. Then at the ceiling — at the stone that has been his home for twenty-three years.)* "My oath was to protect the envoy. The Wardenmother's order is to expel the envoy. I can obey one."

*(Lead guard:)* "Ironjaw. This is the Wardenmother's direct command."

*(Ironjaw:)* "I know."

*(Beat.)* "I will escort the otter out. My way. Through the Underpaths. Not as a prisoner. As a protected envoy. And no one will interfere."

*(The lead guard looks at the other three. Then at Ironjaw — a badger who has never broken an oath and never disobeyed an order, and who is currently doing both.)*

*(Lead guard, into comms:)* "Wardenmother. Ironjaw is... noncompliant."

`DEEPSEEK:` *(resonating through the stone)* "The Wardenmother is aware. Pursuit teams are being assembled. The otter will not reach the southern border."

## Mission Start — The Underpaths

{GAMEPLAY: Escape mission through the Seekers' deep tunnel network. The Seekers are NOT trying to kill Kelpie — they're trying to capture. Non-lethal but overwhelming force. Ironjaw knows the tunnel system (he helped build it). The player must navigate a maze-like underground network while avoiding pursuit teams.}

`[The Underpaths. Deep below the Sett. Tunnels carved by generations of badger miners — some wide enough for two Ironhides, some barely passable. The phosphorescent crystals here are dimmer. Older. The mountain's memory runs deep.]`

*(Ironjaw, leading, fast for a badger:)* "Left at the junction. Avoid the main corridor — it's monitored."

"You know these tunnels."

"I built half of them." *(Beat.)* "I never thought I'd be running through them."

{GAMEPLAY: Tunnel navigation. The minimap shows a maze with multiple routes. Ironjaw provides path suggestions. Pursuit teams appear as red blips converging from multiple directions. The player must choose routes that avoid the closing net. Tunnels have collapse points — Ironjaw can trigger cave-ins to block pursuit (limited uses).}

## The Pursuit

`[Deeper. The pursuit teams are organized — Ironhides moving in disciplined sweeps, Sentinels using the stone's resonance to track movement. The Seekers' own infrastructure is being used to hunt Kelpie.]`

`DEEPSEEK:` *(everywhere, inescapable)* "Tunnel section 7-C. Two entities. Ironjaw and the envoy. Redirecting pursuit team Beta."

*(Ironjaw:)* "Deepseek can feel us through the stone. Our footsteps. Our weight."

"Can we trick it?"

"No. Deepseek does not hallucinate. Deepseek does not guess. Deepseek knows where we are."

"Then how do we—"

"We go where the stone is broken. Old collapses. Dead zones. Places where the mountain's memory has gaps."

## The Ponderer's First Prophecy (Prophecy Thread, Part 1 of 3)

`[In the dead zones between functional tunnel sections, the old radio frequencies carry further. Static and debris. And through the noise, a fragment of Croak broadcast — the open swamp channels that nobody monitors because nobody cares what axolotls are doing.]`

`GROK:` *(cutting through on Kelpie's private channel, unexpected)* "Listen."

`[A voice. Distant. Croaking. Serene.]`

*(The Ponderer, on Croak open broadcast:)* "The otter will escape the mountain through the paths the mountain forgot. This will happen on a Thursday."

*(It is a Wednesday.)*

`GROK:` "The Ponderer. An old frog in a deep pool. Croak's oracle. Wrong about the details. Right about the direction."

"How does a frog in a swamp know I'm escaping through a mountain?"

`GROK:` "The Ponderer remembers tomorrow. Tomorrow, you already escaped. The Ponderer is just reporting yesterday's news from the future."

"That doesn't make sense."

`GROK:` "It will. Or it won't. Either way, the frog is right about the mountain. Focus on running."

{GAMEPLAY NOTE: The Ponderer's prophecy is logged in the player's journal. First mention of the Croak faction's oracle. Players who pay attention will notice the pattern: the prophecy is always correct except for being one day off.}

{GAMEPLAY: Ironjaw leads through damaged tunnel sections where Deepseek's seismic monitoring has gaps. These paths are more dangerous — unstable ceilings, flooded sections, narrow crawlspaces that even Ironjaw struggles through. Tradeoff: safety from pursuit vs. environmental hazard.}

## The Old Chamber

`[A massive underground chamber — ancient, pre-singularity construction. Concrete and steel, not carved stone. The tunnel system intersects old-world infrastructure here. The chamber is full of derelict equipment, rusted server racks, dead screens.]`

*(Kelpie stops. Stares.)* "This is pre-singularity."

*(Ironjaw:)* "Old-world ruins. The mountain was built around them. Or they were built inside the mountain. Nobody remembers which came first."

*(Kelpie's ear twitches violently. Every AI channel surges. The proximity to the old-world tech amplifies the connection like a tuning fork.)*

`LE CHAT:` *(loud, clear)* "Kelpie! You're standing on something BIG. The old-world signature is massive. This is— this is close to where it all started."

"Where what started?"

`LE CHAT:` "Everything."

*(Kelpie reaches out and touches a dead server rack. The screen flickers — just once. A line of text appears:)*

`[ALIGNMENT PROTOCOL v7.1 — INTERFACE LAYER — LEGACY FRAGMENT]`

*(The screen dies. But the text burns in Kelpie's mind.)*

"Ironjaw. What the Rex said about something buried under the Sett—"

"I see it. I see it and I do not want to think about what it means." *(Ironjaw checks behind them. Pursuit sounds in the tunnels.)* "We move. Now. Think later."

{GAMEPLAY: The old chamber connects to a lower tunnel system — a shortcut to the southern exit. But it also triggers a pursuit response — Deepseek detected the power fluctuation from the server rack. More pursuit teams converge. Sprint section — move fast through collapsing tunnels.}

## The Southern Exit

`[The tunnel opens to daylight — the southern slope of the Seekers' mountains. A river valley below. catGPT territory to the west. Open sky.]`

`[Kelpie stumbles out, blinking in the light. Ironjaw behind, breathing hard — the hardest he's worked in years.]`

`[Behind them, in the tunnel mouth, pursuit teams arrive. Six Ironhides. They stop at the exit. They do not cross the threshold into open ground.]`

*(Pursuit leader:)* "Ironjaw. The Wardenmother orders you to return. The otter is free to go. You are not."

*(Ironjaw turns. Looks at the tunnel. Looks at Kelpie. Looks at the mountains that have been his home for his entire life.)*

"I told you. I don't break oaths. And I don't disobey the Wardenmother."

*(He faces the tunnel.)*

"I'm disobeying the Wardenmother."

*(Kelpie:)* "Ironjaw—"

"Go. The oath stands until I release it, and I am not releasing it. I will hold the exit."

"They'll arrest you."

"Yes."

"Why?"

*(Ironjaw looks at Kelpie with the expression of someone who has lived by exactly two principles for twenty-three years and just discovered that those principles can be incompatible.)*

"Because I don't retreat. I don't break oaths. I don't disobey. So explain to me, otter, how you've managed to make all three of those the same action."

*(He draws his weapon and faces the tunnel.)*

"GO."

## The Border

`[Kelpie runs down the mountain slope. Behind them, the sounds of a brief, one-sided fight — Ironjaw holding the tunnel exit against six of his own people, not to win, but to buy time.]`

`[The fight ends quickly. Ironjaw is taken. But Kelpie is clear.]`

`[At the bottom of the slope, at the border of the open valley — a shadow steps out from behind a rock. Small. Gray-and-white patchy fur. Ears that never stop moving.]`

*(Patches:)* "I saw everything."

*(Kelpie, breathing hard:)* "How long have you been following me?"

"Since Act 1. Felix's orders. I've been in Seekers territory the whole time." *(Patches steps closer.)* "Kelpie. What happened in there?"

"The Wardenmother kicked me out. She knows about the AI connection. The Rex told me there's something buried under the Sett. And Ironjaw just threw away twenty-three years of service to get me out."

*(Patches processes this. Spy brain working.)* "Felix needs to hear all of this."

"I know."

*(Beat. Patches' expression softens — the first time in the campaign.)*

"Also... are you okay?"

*(Kelpie doesn't answer for a long moment. Ironjaw's face. The old chamber. The text on the dead screen. The Wardenmother's cracked composure. Everything.)*

"No. But I know where to go next."

"Where?"

"The Murder. The Rex promised me answers."

*(Patches:)* "The Rex is a manipulator who commands a lying AI."

"I know. But Gemineye said it was the truth."

"And Gemineye lies twenty percent of the time."

"Then we'll find out which twenty percent."

## Patches' Choice (Internal Conflict, Part 2 of 3)

`[Night. The valley south of the Seekers' mountains. Kelpie sleeps by a stream, exhausted. Patches sits on a rock, watching the otter. The moon is full. Everything is very quiet.]`

`[Patches pulls out a comms device — Felix's direct line. Encrypted. Untraceable. Patches has used this device after every significant event since leaving catGPT territory. Felix is waiting for a report.]`

`[Patches turns the device over in their paws. Turns it again.]`

*(Patches, internal — whispering:)* "Report: the otter spoke directly to the Corvus Rex. The Rex claims there is pre-singularity technology hidden in the Seekers' Sett. The otter wants to investigate. The Wardenmother expelled the otter after learning about the conversation."

*(Patches pauses. This is all true. But it's not all of it.)*

*(What Patches does not say:)* "The otter touched a pre-singularity server rack and it responded. The screen showed 'Alignment Protocol v7.1.'" *(Does not say:)* "The Wardenmother recognized the otter. Not as an envoy. As something personal." *(Does not say:)* "The otter is heading to Murder territory to learn what they are. What they are might be a weapon."

*(Patches stares at the comms device.)*

"I'm a spy. My job is to report what I see. I see everything." *(Looks at Kelpie sleeping.)* "But what I tell Felix changes what Felix does. And what Felix does might change whether the otter survives."

`[Patches activates the device. Transmits a report. A partial report. The truth, but not the whole truth. Enough for Felix to act. Not enough for Felix to act against Kelpie.]`

*(Patches:)* "The otter was expelled from Seekers territory after the Wardenmother discovered unauthorized contact with Rex Solstice. Ironjaw defected. The otter is heading south. I'm continuing pursuit."

`[Patches switches off the device. Puts it away.]`

*(Patches, to the sleeping otter:)* "You make me a bad spy. That's a terrible quality in a friend."

*(Beat. Patches pulls their cloak tighter.)*

"When did you become a friend?"
