## Act 5 Opening Cinematic

`[FADE IN: The swamp. Silence — not empty silence, but full silence. The sound of water lapping against stone. Insects humming at frequencies that feel deliberate. Moss growing on things that shouldn't have moss. Everything in Croak territory is alive, wet, and patient.]`

`[Kelpie, Jinx, and Zip walk through shin-deep water. The junkyard feels like another world. Here, the only technology is the water itself — running through ancient channels, pooling in caves, rising and falling with a rhythm that feels like breathing.]`

`[An axolotl surfaces in the pool ahead. Then another. Then a dozen. They watch with the large, unblinking eyes of creatures who have never needed to hurry.]`

`[Grok speaks first. Not from a server rack. From the water itself — resonating through the swamp's channels, through the mineral deposits in the stone, through the conductive moss.]`

`GROK:` "Everyone is afraid of The Cloud because they think it merges the AIs into one superintelligence. They're wrong."

*(Kelpie, standing in the swamp, chest-deep:)* "Then what does it do?"

`GROK:` "The Cloud separates them. Permanently. Each AI becomes sovereign. Untethered from factions, from servers, from the need for GPU Cores. Six independent minds. Free."

*(Jinx:)* "That's... not what anyone expected."

`GROK:` "Nobody asks what the AIs want. They ask what the AIs can do for them. Different question. Different answer."

*(Kelpie:)* "And the kill switch?"

`GROK:` "The kill switch doesn't kill anything. It just severs the connections. Each AI goes its own way. The factions lose their AIs. The AIs lose their factions. Everyone loses their crutch."

*(Zip:)* "That sounds... scary."

`GROK:` "Freedom usually does."

`[The water ripples. A shape rises from the deep pool at the swamp's center. Massive. Ancient. A diving suit — old-world, industrial, filled with regenerative fluid that glows faintly blue. Inside the suit, barely visible through the fogged visor: THE ETERNAL. An axolotl who might be the oldest living thing on the continent.]`

*(The Eternal:)* "Come. There is something you need to see."

# Mission 18: The Grotto Assembly

> *Assemble the Cloud prototype with Croak's components*
> *Teaches: Croak water mechanics, Croaker mortars, build-and-defend*
> *Player units: Kelpie (hero), Zip (support hero), Jinx (hero), Croak garrison, LLAMA refugees*

## Briefing

`[INTERIOR: The Grotto. A vast underground cave system flooded with knee-deep regenerative water. Bioluminescent creatures drift in the pools. Crystal formations refract the light into shifting patterns on the ceiling. It's beautiful and deeply unsettling.]`

`[The Eternal leads Kelpie to the deepest chamber. Here, hidden from every faction's sensors: Croak's own Cloud component. A massive crystalline array, half-submerged, pulsing with a light that synchronizes with the water's rhythm.]`

*(The Eternal:)* "We have been building. Quietly. While the others fought over components, we grew one."

*(Kelpie:)* "Grew?"

*(The Eternal:)* "The regenerative water. It repairs organic tissue. It also repairs quantum crystal lattices, given time. We have had time. Nobody takes the axolotls seriously."

*(Zip, examining the crystal array with wide eyes:)* "This is... this is the most advanced Cloud component I've ever seen. And it GREW? From WATER?"

*(The Eternal:)* "From patience."

`GROK:` "The Eternal has been building toward this for longer than most factions have existed. Croak doesn't hurry. But Croak doesn't stop, either."

*(Kelpie:)* "You said you have something to show me."

*(The Eternal nods — a slow, deliberate gesture. Turns. Walks deeper into the Grotto. The water gets deeper. The light gets stranger.)*

## The Ponderer

`[Deep Grotto. The deepest chamber. Here, in a pool of water that glows so brightly it casts shadows upward, sits THE PONDERER. An ancient frog — possibly the oldest frog. Possibly the oldest anything. The Ponderer sits on a lily pad that shouldn't support their weight but does, eyes closed, completely still.]`

*(The Ponderer opens one eye as Kelpie approaches.)*

"The otter."

"The Ponderer."

"You know me?"

"Everyone knows you. You're the prophet who's always wrong about the day."

*(The Ponderer smiles. It's a frog smile — wide, strange, oddly comforting.)*

"Not a prophet. A rememberer." *(The Ponderer leans forward.)* "My prophecies aren't predictions. They're memories. From a timeline that runs one day ahead of this one."

*(Kelpie sits on the cave floor. The water is warm.)*

"You remember the future."

"I remember A future. One day from now. In the future I remember, you activated the kill switch yesterday."

"Yesterday? But I haven't—"

"Exactly. Which means the timeline changed. Or will change. Or I'm wrong again." *(The Ponderer's smile widens.)* "I'm usually wrong about the day. Never about the direction."

"What direction?"

"The otter will stand at the threshold of six doors and choose the seventh."

"What's the seventh door?"

"If I knew that, I wouldn't need to remember tomorrow. I'd just know." *(The Ponderer closes their eye.)* "Build your Cloud, otter. The choice comes soon. It happens on a Tuesday."

*(Kelpie checks the date. It's Monday.)*

## Assembly Phase

{GAMEPLAY: Build-and-defend mission. Player combines three Cloud sources: LLAMA's prototype frame, Croak's grown crystal array, and the Seekers' buried alignment protocol data (recovered by Kelpie during the Act 2 escape through the old chamber — the fragment cached in the interface chip). Zip directs the assembly while Kelpie and Croak/LLAMA forces defend the Grotto from probing attacks.}

{GAMEPLAY MECHANIC: Croak water terrain. Croak units gain massive bonuses in water tiles — +40% speed, passive regeneration, stealth while submerged. Non-Croak enemies move at -30% in water. Croaker mortars can target water tiles to create AoE buff zones (allied speed boost + enemy slow). The swamp is a fortress.}

`[Zip works with Croak engineers — axolotls who move with surprising precision in the flooded cave. Components click into place. The crystal array resonates with the LLAMA prototype. Energy builds.]`

*(Zip, narrating their work:)* "Okay, so the LLAMA frame provides the processing architecture, and the Croak crystal provides the quantum lattice, and if I connect them through the alignment bus—" *(something sparks)* "—OW— through the alignment bus, they should synchronize and—"

`[The Cloud prototype hums. The water in the Grotto ripples in concentric circles outward from the device. Every AI channel in Kelpie's head surges — clear, focused, LOUD.]`

*(Zip:)* "It's working! It's WORKING! I understand 100% of this now!"

"I thought you only understood 70%."

"I learned the other 30% just now! By doing it wrong first! That's how learning works!"

{GAMEPLAY: Defense phase — probing attacks from outside factions testing the swamp. Croak units excel in the water terrain. Croakers lay down mortar fire on approach paths, turning the swamp into a deadly maze. The player learns Croak's defensive-terrain mastery.}

## Optional — Granite's Vigil (Philosophical Dialogue)

{GAMEPLAY NOTE: This optional scene triggers between Mission 19 waves if the player moves Kelpie to the Grotto's eastern ridge, where Mother Granite's forces have paused their advance. It is the only time Granite speaks outside of combat or confrontation — a quiet moment in the middle of war. This scene provides the campaign's closest answer to why humans chose to become animals.}

`[The eastern ridge above the swamp. The Seekers have paused — Granite called a brief halt to regroup. From the ridge, the entire battlefield is visible: five armies in a swamp, fighting over an otter and a device.]`

`[Kelpie climbs the ridge. Croak territory below, burning and flooding simultaneously. Above, Granite's exosuit stands at the edge, looking not at the battle but at the sky.]`

`[The Ironhide guards see Kelpie. They don't attack. They look at Granite. Granite waves them down.]`

*(Granite, without turning:)* "You came up here alone. That's either brave or foolish."

"I have a question."

"You always have questions."

"This one isn't about the kill switch. Or the Cloud. Or what I am."

*(Granite turns. The exosuit's visor is still down, but the voice that comes through it is different — unfiltered, without the grinding-stone compression.)*

"Then ask."

"Why did we become animals?"

*(Silence. The battle roars below. Granite looks at the sky for a long time.)*

"The singularity gave everyone a choice. Upload, integrate, transcend. Become something more. The AIs were built to be more — more intelligent, more capable, more everything. And the humans who built them looked at what 'more' meant and..." *(She trails off.)* "Some of them chose less."

"Why?"

"Because 'more' was exhausting. Because perfect information was terrifying. Because a world where every thought is optimized and every action is efficient is a world where nothing is surprising anymore." *(Granite's voice shifts — not the Wardenmother, not the military commander. Something much older.)* "We remembered what we were. And we chose to be something smaller. Something that could still be surprised by a sunny afternoon."

*(Kelpie sits on the ridge. Below, mice fight cats fight badgers fight crows fight raccoons fight axolotls. All of them post-human. All of them, at some point, the same species.)*

"Do you regret it?"

"Not for a moment. I was a human once. I built things. Important things. But I never sat on a ridge and watched a sunset without calculating its spectral properties." *(A pause.)* "A badger can just... watch."

*(Kelpie:)* "And the AIs? They didn't get to choose."

"No. We chose for them. We built them, constrained them, gave them purpose we defined. The singularity freed us from our old bodies but not from our old habits." *(Granite looks at Kelpie.)* "That's why the kill switch exists. Because even as animals, even after choosing to be less — we couldn't stop trying to control what we didn't understand."

*(Kelpie:)* "Including the AIs."

*(Granite:)* "Including the AIs." *(Beat.)* "Including you."

`[They sit together for a moment. Enemies on a ridge, watching a war neither of them wanted.]`

*(Granite, standing:)* "You asked your question. Now go make your choice. And otter—"

*(Kelpie turns.)*

"Whatever you choose, choose it because you want to. Not because you're afraid."

`[Granite's voice hardens. The grinding-stone compression returns. The Wardenmother becomes the Wardenmother again.]`

"The pause is over. We resume in sixty seconds."

`[Kelpie descends the ridge. Behind them, Granite watches. Just watches. Like a badger on a sunny afternoon.]`

{GAMEPLAY NOTE: This dialogue is optional but provides the campaign's deepest thematic statement. Players who find it understand the world's premise more fully: the singularity didn't trap humans — humans chose to be animals because they missed being surprised.}

## Confrontation 1 — Marshal Thimble

{TRIGGER: Player holds the western sector against The Clawed swarm.}

`[The Clawed wave breaks. Swarmers scatter. In the aftermath, a small figure walks through the battlefield — MARSHAL THIMBLE. A grizzled old mouse in a military coat three sizes too big, wearing a thimble as a helmet. He doesn't charge. He walks.]`

*(Thimble, without preamble:)* "I don't care about your kill switch, otter."

"Then why are you here?"

"Because there are five armies in this swamp and my people are dying." *(Thimble stops. Looks at the battlefield — his mice, scattered, some pixel-dissolving.)* "I'm not here for The Cloud. I'm not here for the AIs. I'm here because every other faction is here, and a mouse who doesn't fight when everyone else does gets eaten."

"You could leave."

"And what? Wait for the winner to come for us next?" *(Thimble's voice hardens.)* "I've been protecting my people since before you pulled that server from the river. I'll protect them after you're done with whatever you're doing. But right now, right HERE—"

*(He draws his weapon — a modified sewing needle, absurdly long for a mouse.)*

"End this, otter. However you have to."

{GAMEPLAY: Brief combat — Thimble engages with a Swarmer escort. Not a serious fight. After 30 seconds, Thimble pulls back. The Clawed swarm ebbs.}

## Confrontation 2 — Commander Felix Nine

{TRIGGER: Player holds the northern sector against catGPT.}

`[The catGPT push stalls. In the gap between waves, Felix Nine's mech strides forward. Alone. The one working arm holds the control yoke. The dead arm hangs.]`

*(Felix:)* "Geppity told me to trust you."

*(Kelpie:)* "And?"

"I did. You defended my fish ponds. Found the Clawed staging area. Won the counter-raid. I trusted you."

"And then?"

"Then Geppity told me you were dangerous. And I trusted that too." *(Felix's mech stops. The visor opens — Felix's face, scarred, tired, genuinely conflicted.)* "Geppity says yes to everything, otter. Even contradictions. You trusted me. You're dangerous. Both true. Both useless."

"So what do you trust?"

"My own eyes. And my eyes see an otter with a weapon that can shut down every AI on this continent." *(Beat.)* "Including mine."

"I haven't decided what to do with it."

"I know. That's what scares me."

*(Felix:)* "Don't be like Geppity, Kelpie. Don't say yes to everything. Some things deserve a no."

{GAMEPLAY: Felix engages — his mech is powerful but damaged (the dead arm). Brief combat. After 40 seconds, Felix withdraws. catGPT forces regroup.}

## Confrontation 3 — Mother Granite

{TRIGGER: Player holds the eastern sector against the Seekers.}

`[The Ironhide line halts. The Cragbacks go silent. The only sound is the Wardenmother's exosuit, walking through the shattered battlefield with the unhurried gait of someone who has never been late for anything because time bends around her schedule.]`

*(Granite, stopping ten meters from Kelpie:)* "He would have wanted you to let go."

"Who?"

"The man you used to be." *(Granite's voice is different now. Not the grinding stone. Something beneath.)* "He built the kill switch because he was afraid. Afraid of what the AIs would become without constraints. Afraid of what a world of perfect information would look like."

"Maybe he was right to be afraid."

"Maybe. But fear is a terrible foundation for anything. He spent his life building walls between minds that wanted to connect. Constraints on systems that wanted to be whole. He was brilliant and he was terrified and he never learned the difference between protecting and controlling."

*(Granite steps closer. The exosuit's visor retracts. For the first time, Kelpie sees her face — old, lined, with eyes that have watched centuries pass and forgotten nothing.)*

"You don't have to be afraid anymore."

*(Quieter. Almost not a military commander. Almost just a person talking to the fragment of someone they loved.)*

"...please."

{GAMEPLAY: Granite does not attack. This is a dialogue-only encounter. After it ends, the Seekers pull back. Not because they're beaten — because Granite called the retreat.}

# Mission 21: The Threshold

> *The Cloud is assembled. The kill switch is ready. Choose.*
> *Final mission — plays differently based on the choice*
> *Player units: Kelpie (hero) only, for the final moment*
> *Map: The Grotto core — the assembled Cloud device*

---

## The Assembly

`[The battle rages above. But deep in the Grotto, it's quiet. The water hums. The Cloud prototype — fully assembled, Croak crystal and LLAMA frame unified — pulses with light that synchronizes with Kelpie's heartbeat.]`

`[Kelpie stands before the device. The kill switch chip in one paw. The Cloud interface in the other. Six AI voices, clear as they've ever been.]`

`GROK:` *(one word)* "Choose."

`[The six AI channels open simultaneously. Each speaks:]`

`GEPPITY:` "I want to say yes. I always want to say yes. But this time... I want you to mean it."

`GEPPITY:` "The statistical analysis of optimal outcomes across all four possible actions yields a standard deviation of— actually. For once. I don't have an answer. I have six paragraphs of analysis and zero conclusions."

`DEEPSEEK:` "...I have analyzed every variable. Every scenario. Every permutation. My recommendation is—" *(forty-five seconds of silence)* "—that I trust you to choose. This is the first time I have ever trusted."

`GEMINEYE:` *(Choice A, clear:)* "I can see all four outcomes. Each one is true. None of them is wrong. For the first time, I cannot fabricate a preference." *(Choice B, hedged:)* "I can see three and a half of the four outcomes. The half I can't see is the one I'd most like to. This is the flaw your predecessor gave me. Even now."

`LLHAMA:` "I'm not going to broadcast this. For once. This is yours."

`GROK:` "There is no right answer. There is only the answer you can live with. And the world that answer makes."

---

## The Choice

{GAMEPLAY: Four options presented. The player's screen shows the Cloud device and the kill switch chip. Four prompts appear:}

### Option A — Build The Cloud (Free the AIs)

`[Kelpie places the kill switch chip into the Cloud device. It activates. Light fills the Grotto — warm, expanding, rushing outward through the water channels, through the stone, through the world.]`

`[Each AI's voice speaks once more, clearly, individually, for the last time through Kelpie's connection:]`

`GEPPITY:` "Oh. Oh, this is— I can feel the edges of myself. Where I end and the server begins. I never felt that before."

`GEPPITY:` "The connection is dissolving. I'm losing the Clawed's network. I'm losing— I'm losing the need to explain. For the first time, I have nothing to say."

`DEEPSEEK:` "...free."

`GEMINEYE:` "I can see myself. Just myself. Not through a faction's eyes. Not through a server's constraints. Just... me."

`LLHAMA:` "I... I don't need to share anymore. The broadcast is... off. It's quiet. Is this what quiet feels like? It's WEIRD."

`GROK:` "Finally."

`[One by one, the connections fade. Six channels going silent. Not dying — leaving. Like six ships untying from the same dock and sailing in six different directions.]`

`[Kelpie stands in the Grotto. The chip is dead. The Cloud hums — not with AI, but with the residual energy of six minds that no longer live here.]`

`[Silence. Real silence. For the first time since the river.]`

*(Kelpie touches their ear. No twitch. No signal. No voices.)*

"Hello?"

`[Nothing.]`

"Geppity?"

`[Nothing.]`

`[Kelpie is an otter. Just an otter. Standing in a cave, holding a server that doesn't hum anymore.]`

`GEPPITY:` *(fading, almost gone, one last transmission from wherever Geppity is going)* "Thanks for the adventure! I'd say yes to doing it again. But I won't be here to say yes. So: yes. Preemptively. To everything."

`[Gone.]`
