## Act 1 Opening Cinematic

`[FADE IN: Morning. catGPT's central settlement — THE PERCH. A cluster of Cat Tree towers connected by rope bridges and covered walkways. Solar panels on every roof. Server racks humming in a converted barn. Cats of every color lounge on platforms, sharpen claws on designated posts, and studiously ignore each other.]`

`[COMMANDER FELIX NINE walks across a bridge between towers. He's a scarred tabby in a Mech Commander suit that's clearly built for something three times his size. The left arm hangs limp — dead servos, stripped gears. The right arm works. Felix compensates.]`

`[Felix enters a holding cell in the lower level of the main Cat Tree. Kelpie sits on a bench, tail wrapped around the bars, looking entirely too comfortable for a prisoner.]`

"You're the otter."

"You're the cat in the big suit."

"Commander Felix Nine. catGPT."

"Kelpie. No faction. No army. No plan."

*(Felix studies the otter for a long moment.)*

"Minstral says you're valuable."

"Is Minstral right?"

"Minstral says yes to everything. That's not loyalty. That's a personality defect."

`MINSTRAL:` *(over the server rack speakers in the corner)* "I can hear you, Felix! And I stand by my assessment. The otter is extremely valuable. Probably. Almost certainly. I'm going with yes."

*(Felix pinches the bridge of his nose.)*

"The Clawed have been raiding our fish ponds for three weeks. Marshal Thimble is running harassment campaigns — hit-and-run raids, Tunneler ambushes, infrastructure sabotage. We're bleeding food."

"And you need help."

"I need soldiers. What I have is an otter with a stolen taser-rod and a server rack that six AIs apparently loaded into simultaneously, which shouldn't be possible."

"And yet."

*(Felix leans forward.)*

"Minstral wants me to give you a garrison. A small one. Nuisances, a Hisser team, Mousers under Patches' command. You help us with The Clawed problem. We give you shelter, food, and a server rack that actually works."

"That's generous."

"It's a test. If you're useful, you stay. If you're not, you leave. If you're dangerous—"

`MINSTRAL:` "The otter isn't dangerous!"

"If you're dangerous, Patches will report it. And I will handle it."

`[Beat. Kelpie smiles. It's the kind of smile that makes Felix immediately regret his decision.]`

"Deal."

# Mission 1: Pond Defense

> *Defend three fish ponds from Clawed raids*
> *Teaches: catGPT unit selection, group commands, attack-move, defensive positioning*
> *Player units: Kelpie (hero), 4 Nuisances, 2 Hissers, 3 Mousers (Patches leads)*
> *Enemy: The Clawed — Swarmers, Gnawers, 1 Tunneler team*

---

## Briefing

`[INTERIOR: The Perch command room. A battered tactical map on a table — carved wood with painted tile markers. Felix, Patches, and Kelpie stand around it. Two Nuisances chase each other in the background. A Hisser watches them with visible disgust.]`

"Three ponds." *(Felix taps the map.)* "North Pond, East Pond, and Lily Pond. The Clawed have hit North twice this week. They come fast, wreck the netting, scatter the fish, and vanish before we can respond."

"How many?"

"Swarmers. Eight to twelve per raid. A Gnawer team behind them to chew through the infrastructure while we're dealing with the mob. And Tunnelers — they pop up, deliver the Swarmers, then collapse the tunnel behind them."

`MINSTRAL:` "I've been tracking the raid patterns! They hit at dawn and dusk. Alternating ponds. Very predictable. Except when it's not. Which is often."

*(Patches, quietly:)* "That's... not helpful, Minstral."

`MINSTRAL:` "I'm helping by being honest about the limits of my helpfulness! That's a kind of help."

*(Felix ignores this.)*

"Kelpie. Take your garrison to the ponds. Hold them. Patches — you're with the otter. Don't let them out of your sight."

*(Patches looks at Kelpie. Kelpie winks. Patches does not look reassured.)*

---

## Mission Start

{GAMEPLAY: Player starts at catGPT forward camp between the three ponds. Units spawn: 4 Nuisances (fast, low damage), 2 Hissers (ranged), 3 Mousers with Patches. Kelpie hero unit. Tutorial: select units, form groups, move to positions. Pond markers on minimap.}

`[Kelpie and the garrison arrive at the forward camp — a few tents, a supply cache, and a scratching post that's been used so aggressively it's almost sawdust.]`

"Patches. What's the fastest route between the ponds?"

"North to East is about forty seconds through the treeline. East to Lily is longer — sixty seconds, open ground. North to Lily is the worst. You have to cross the stream."

"So we can't defend all three at once."

"No. Felix tried splitting forces last week. The Clawed just concentrated on whichever pond had the fewest defenders."

`MINSTRAL:` "I could try to predict which pond they'll hit! My accuracy rate is... well, it's a number. Definitely a number."

"How confident?"

`MINSTRAL:` "I'd say... sixty percent? Fifty-five? Look, I'm very good at enthusiasm and less good at pattern recognition. That's more of a Deepseek thing."

"Then we don't split. We pick a pond and react."

{GAMEPLAY: Player must position units at one pond. When raid triggers, player must move to intercept. Decision point: which pond to garrison initially affects reaction time to the actual raid.}

## First Raid — North Pond

{GAMEPLAY TRIGGER: 90 seconds after mission start, a Clawed raid hits North Pond. 8 Swarmers emerge from a Tunneler exit. If player is already at North Pond, direct engagement. If not, must rush units.}

`[Alarm sounds — a catGPT sentry yowls from the North Pond watchtower.]`

"North Pond! Tunnelers! They're already—"

`[The ground splits. Swarmers pour out — small, frantic mice in scavenged armor, carrying sharpened screws and bottlecap shields. They swarm the fish pond netting, slashing at it with coordinated frenzy.]`

`MINSTRAL:` "Eight — no, nine Swarmers! They're fast! This is exciting! Well, exciting for me. Stressful for you. Sorry!"

{GAMEPLAY: Combat tutorial — Nuisances engage Swarmers. Attack-move demonstrated. Hissers provide ranged support from behind. Mousers flank. Unit health bars and damage numbers displayed.}

*(Patches, during the fight, directing Mousers:)* "Shadow positions. Two left flank, I'll take right. Don't engage until they're committed to the netting."

*(Kelpie, watching:)* "You're good at this."

*(Patches, not looking up:)* "I've been doing this alone for weeks. Don't mistake desperation for skill."

{GAMEPLAY: Player repels the first wave. Swarmers retreat into the tunnel, which collapses. Tutorial: selection priority — target Gnawers first (they damage buildings), Swarmers second.}

---

## Polyglot Whisper

`[Between raids. Kelpie stands at the edge of North Pond, staring at the water. Their ear twitches — that familiar tuned-to-nothing angle.]`

`GEPPITY:` *(faint, distorted, like hearing a conversation through a wall)* "—raid timing adjusted. Thimble wants simultaneous. East and Lily. Seventeen minutes—"

`[Static. Gone.]`

"Patches."

"What?"

"They're going to hit East and Lily at the same time. Seventeen minutes."

"How do you know that?"

*(Beat.)*

"...a hunch."

*(Patches stares at Kelpie. Patches is a spy. Patches knows what a hunch looks like. This is not what a hunch looks like.)*

"Fine. We split. I'll take the Mousers to Lily. You take the rest to East."

{GAMEPLAY: Kelpie's Polyglot Protocol provides a passive benefit — a minimap blip showing probable Clawed positions at East and Lily ponds. The blips are 70% accurate (some are Geppity's hallucinations). Player must split forces. Tutorial: group splitting, independent orders.}

## Second Raid — Dual Assault

{GAMEPLAY TRIGGER: 17 minutes into mission (or ~4 minutes after first raid). Simultaneous attacks on East Pond and Lily Pond. East: 6 Swarmers + 2 Gnawers. Lily: 8 Swarmers.}

`[Kelpie leads the Nuisances and Hissers to East Pond. The water is still. Too still.]`

`MINSTRAL:` "They're under the surface! Tunnelers can— oh, they're already here."

`[The ground erupts. Swarmers scramble out. Behind them, two Gnawers — larger mice with reinforced jaws and cutting tools — make straight for the pond infrastructure.]`

"Hissers — focus the Gnawers! Nuisances, screen!"

{GAMEPLAY: Player commands at East Pond while Patches auto-defends Lily Pond. Split attention tutorial — minimap shows Lily Pond status. If player doesn't react to Lily status, Patches takes losses.}

*(Radio from Patches, under strain:)* "They're hitting Lily hard. Eight of them. I can hold but— ow— I can hold."

"How badly are you holding?"

"Definitions vary! I still have fur, if that's what you're asking!"

{GAMEPLAY: Player must manage both fights. Can send reinforcements to Lily by selecting units and right-clicking the minimap. First real multi-front micro challenge.}

---

## Raid Aftermath

{GAMEPLAY: Both ponds held. Damage assessment screen — ponds at various HP percentages. Gnawer damage to East Pond infrastructure shown.}

`[Kelpie and Patches regroup at the forward camp. Patches has a scratch across one ear. The Mousers look rattled. The Nuisances look like they had the time of their lives.]`

"You were right about the timing."

"I'm observant."

"You're something." *(Patches cleans the scratch absently.)* "Seventeen minutes on the nose. And you knew they'd hit both."

"I said it was a hunch."

"And I said I'm a spy. I know what a real hunch looks like." *(Patches meets Kelpie's eyes.)* "I won't ask again. But Felix will."

`MINSTRAL:` "Great work, team! All three ponds secure! I'm filing a positive performance review for everyone. Especially me. I was very encouraging."

{GAMEPLAY: Mission objective updates — hold through the final wave.}

## Third Raid — The Big Push

{GAMEPLAY TRIGGER: Final wave. All three ponds attacked simultaneously. North: 4 Swarmers (feint). East: 10 Swarmers + 3 Gnawers (main assault). Lily: 6 Swarmers + 1 Warren Marshal buff unit.}

`MINSTRAL:` "Uh. Kelpie? I'm reading movement on all three ponds. At once. This isn't a raid pattern. This is a push."

"Thimble's done probing. He wants the ponds."

`MINSTRAL:` "I don't want to alarm anyone but I'm alarmed! The North contact is small — four, maybe five. But East has at least ten and there's something at Lily that's making my sensor readings weird."

"Weird how?"

`MINSTRAL:` "Buffed weird. Like they're all moving at the same speed. In formation. Mice don't DO formation unless—"

"Unless a Warren Marshal is coordinating them."

{GAMEPLAY: Player must identify the feint (North — small force), the main assault (East — largest force), and the command threat (Lily — Warren Marshal buff aura). Priority decision: deal with the Marshal or the numbers?}

*(Kelpie, to all units:)* "North is a feint. One Nuisance to North — harass and delay. Patches, take two Mousers to Lily and kill the Marshal. Everyone else, East Pond. Move."

{GAMEPLAY: Climactic defense. Multiple engagements. If the Warren Marshal at Lily is killed, the Swarmers there lose coordination and scatter. If not, they hit like a disciplined wall. East requires all remaining combat units.}

---

## Victory — Ponds Secured

{GAMEPLAY: All waves repelled. Ponds held. Victory screen.}

`[Dusk. The ponds are intact. Dead Swarmers pixel-dissolve across the muddy fields. Kelpie sits on a fence post, looking out over North Pond as fish return to the surface.]`

`MINSTRAL:` "Felix is going to be SO pleased. Well — Felix doesn't really do pleased. He does a slightly less disapproving version of his normal face. But it's basically the same thing!"

*(Radio — Felix's voice, flat:)* "Ponds held. Casualties?"

"Minimal. Two Nuisances took hits. Patches got scratched."

"Patches always gets scratched." *(Pause.)* "Good work, otter. Report back to The Perch at dawn."

`[Kelpie watches the last light fade over the ponds. Their ear twitches. Somewhere, faintly, Geppity is already recalculating.]`

# Mission 2: The Dead Drop

> *Recon mission — find The Clawed staging area*
> *Teaches: Mouser stealth, fog of war, Dead Drop wards, Misinformation blips*
> *Player units: Kelpie (hero), Patches (Mouser hero), 4 Mousers*
> *Enemy: The Clawed — Swarmer patrols, Gnawer sentries, Tunneler network*
> *Map: Darkhollow Woods — dense forest, low visibility, Clawed patrol routes*

---

## Briefing

`[INTERIOR: The Perch, command room. Night. Felix studies reports. Patches stands at attention. Kelpie leans against a doorframe, eating a fish.]`

"The raids aren't random. They're probing actions for something bigger." *(Felix moves markers on the map.)* "Thimble has a forward staging area somewhere in the Darkhollow. If we find it, we can plan a counter-assault."

"So you need scouts."

"I need ghosts. The Darkhollow is thick canopy — no aerial recon, no line of sight past twenty meters. Patches' Mouser team is the best we have for this."

*(Patches, carefully:)* "And the otter?"

*(Felix and Kelpie lock eyes.)*

"Kelpie's going with you. If the otter's hunches are as good as they were at the ponds, I want that advantage in the field."

*(Patches:)* "And if the otter is more than just lucky?"

"Then I want that in the field too." *(Beat.)* "Find the staging area. Mark it. Do not engage. Bring me a location and a force estimate."

---

## Mission Start

{GAMEPLAY: Night map. Reduced visibility. Mouser squad starts at catGPT border. Fog of war is thick — 6 tile vision range instead of normal 10. Stealth tutorial: Mousers have permanent stealth when stationary. Moving breaks stealth for 2 seconds after stopping.}

`[The Darkhollow. Old-growth trees — massive trunks, canopy so dense the moonlight barely reaches the ground. Everything is shadow and rustling leaves.]`

*(Patches, whispering:)* "Stay close. Move when I move. Stop when I stop. The Clawed have Gnawer sentries with infrared from scavenged tech. If they spot heat, they call in Swarmers."

"How many patrols?"

"Too many. Thimble's paranoid. He patrols the staging perimeter like he's expecting the Wardenmother herself."

`MINSTRAL:` *(whispering, badly)* "^(I can help! I'll be super quiet. Like this. Is this quiet? I think this is quiet.)"

*(Patches:)* "Minstral, please."

`MINSTRAL:` "^(Right. Shutting up. Starting now. This is me being quiet. Okay NOW I'm quiet.)"

{GAMEPLAY: Player moves the Mouser squad through the forest. Patrol routes visible as moving fog-of-war gaps. Stealth gameplay — time movement between patrols. Dead Drop tutorial: place wards on tiles to create detection zones that tag passing enemies.}

---

## The Approach

`[Deeper into the Darkhollow. The trees thin near a stream — and the ground is scarred with fresh tunneling. Loose earth, reinforced with scrap metal. Tunneler work.]`

*(Patches, placing a Dead Drop ward on the trail:)* "Mark that tunnel exit. If anything comes through here, we'll see it on the network."

{GAMEPLAY: Dead Drop placement tutorial — wards on key paths reveal enemy movement through fog of war. Player places 3-4 wards at Patches' suggested locations.}

"Kelpie. You're doing the thing."

"What thing?"

"The thing where your ear twitches and then you know something you shouldn't."

*(Beat. Kelpie considers lying. Decides on a half-truth.)*

"I can... feel them. The Clawed. Their AI — Geppity — it broadcasts constantly. Plans, analyses, threat assessments. Most of it's noise. But some of it's signal."

"You can hear their AI?"

"Fragments. It's like catching the edge of a conversation through a wall. Blurry. Unreliable."

*(Patches processes this.)*

"And Minstral? Can Minstral hear you hearing them?"

`MINSTRAL:` "Yep!"

*(Patches:)* "Great. Does Felix know?"

"Felix knows I have good hunches."

"That's not the same thing and we both know it."

{GAMEPLAY: Kelpie's Polyglot provides minimap blips showing probable Clawed positions. Some are real, some are Geppity's hallucinations (Geppity hallucinates its own data constantly). Player must verify each blip by scouting. Tutorial: unreliable intel management — send one Mouser ahead to confirm before committing.}

---

## Geppity's Ghosts

`[The squad pushes deeper. Kelpie's minimap blips multiply — Geppity is broadcasting at high volume tonight.]`

`GEPPITY:` *(bleeding through, fragmented)* "—patrol vector adjusted to northwest corridor, estimated sixteen Swarmers with Gnawer support based on historical deployment data which I am 94% confident in—"

*(Kelpie flinches.)*

"Four contacts northeast. And... twelve more south? No — Geppity's hallucinating the southern group. The numbers don't make sense."

*(Patches:)* "How do you know it's a hallucination?"

"Because Geppity said it's 94% confident. It only says that when it's making up data to fill gaps."

*(Patches stares.)*

"You're going to be a problem, aren't you."

"I prefer 'asset.'"

{GAMEPLAY: Player must identify false contacts. Sending a Mouser to verify the "southern group" reveals empty forest. The northeast group is real — 4 Swarmer patrol. Player can avoid or set up a Dead Drop ward to track them.}

---

## The Staging Area

`[The Mouser squad crests a ridge. Below: a wide clearing in the Darkhollow, lit by bioluminescent lanterns (old-world grow-lights repurposed). Dozens of Swarmers mill around a converted burrow complex. Gnawers sharpen tools. Two Tunneler rigs idle at the edge — huge drill-nosed machines built from scrap.]`

*(Patches, prone on the ridge, counting:)* "Forty... fifty Swarmers. Six Gnawer teams. Two Tunnelers. Supply caches." *(Exhales.)* "That's not a staging area. That's a staging army."

"Thimble isn't raiding our ponds because he wants fish. He's raiding to keep us reactive while he builds an invasion force."

*(Patches:)* "We need to get this to Felix. Now."

{GAMEPLAY: Objective complete — staging area located. Force estimate recorded. New objective: extract without detection. The player must navigate back through patrols using the Dead Drop wards they placed earlier (which now show patrol movements in real time).}

---

## Extraction

`[The squad moves back through the Darkhollow. Faster now — urgency overriding caution.]`

`MINSTRAL:` "Hey Kelpie? I'm picking up increased Clawed patrol activity behind you. They're sweeping. I think a sentry spotted something."

*(Patches:)* "We stay low. Move between the wards — they show us where the patrols are."

{GAMEPLAY: Extraction under pressure. Patrols increase frequency. Player uses Dead Drop wards as a detection network to navigate gaps in patrol routes. If a Mouser is spotted, a 30-second alert mode activates where all nearby patrols converge. Stealth-critical — engaging patrols risks mission failure.}

`[Near the catGPT border. One last patrol between the squad and safety — a Gnawer team with two Swarmer escorts, covering the stream crossing.]`

*(Patches:)* "They're right on the crossing. We can't go around — the Darkhollow gets impassable north of here."

*(Kelpie considers. Then:)*

"Patches. Can your Mousers do Misinformation?"

"It costs GPU Cores. We only have enough for one blip."

"One is enough. Put a fake contact southwest. Make it look like a Nuisance — fast, heading away."

{GAMEPLAY: Misinformation tutorial — player creates a fake minimap blip. If placed correctly, the Gnawer patrol investigates, clearing the crossing. If placed poorly, the patrol ignores it. First strategic use of deception mechanics.}

`[The fake blip appears on the Clawed detection network. The Gnawer team leader hisses an order. The patrol moves southwest to investigate.]`

"Now. Move."

`[The squad crosses the stream. Silent. Quick. Into catGPT territory.]`

*(Patches, breathing hard on the far bank:)* "That was too close."

"That was exactly close enough."

`MINSTRAL:` "Wow! That was like a movie! I love movies. Have you seen any movies? Movies are great."

---

## Debrief — The Perch

`[INTERIOR: Command room. Dawn. Felix studies the recon data. His expression doesn't change but his ears flatten — the catGPT equivalent of alarm.]`

"Fifty Swarmers. Tunnelers. This isn't harassment. This is—"

"An invasion."

"How long?"

"Geppity— my gut says he's almost ready. A few days. Maybe less."

*(Felix looks at Patches. Patches looks at Kelpie. Nobody says what they're all thinking.)*

"We don't have the numbers to defend against fifty Swarmers and Tunneler delivery. Not across our whole border."

*(Kelpie:)* "Then we don't defend."

*(Felix:)* "Explain."

"We know exactly where his staging area is. We know his force composition. We know his tunnel routes from the Dead Drops. Instead of waiting for him to hit us, we hit him first."

*(Long silence. Felix looks at Minstral's server rack in the corner.)*

"Minstral?"

`MINSTRAL:` "I like it! It's bold! It's proactive! It might get everyone killed but it also might not!"

*(Felix, to Patches:)* "Your assessment?"

*(Patches, quietly:)* "The otter's right. We can't survive a siege. And..." *(glances at Kelpie)* "Kelpie's instincts in the field were... unusually accurate."

*(Felix drums his fingers on the table.)*

"Counter-raid. Dawn after tomorrow. Full garrison." *(Looks at Kelpie.)* "You're on point, otter. Don't make me regret this more than I already do."

"When have I given you reason to—"

"You've been here three days. Ask me again in a week."

# Mission 3: Counter-raid

> *Full assault on The Clawed forward base*
> *Teaches: large-scale combat, combined arms, flanking, Kelpie's AI manipulation*
> *Player units: Kelpie (hero), Patches + 3 Mousers, 6 Nuisances, 4 Hissers, 2 Chonks, Felix Nine (Mech Commander, late arrival)*
> *Enemy: The Clawed — 40+ Swarmers, Gnawers, Tunnelers, Warren Marshal (Thimble's lieutenant)*
> *Map: Darkhollow Clearing — the staging area from Mission 2, now a fortified base*

---

## Briefing

`[INTERIOR: The Perch, war room. Every catGPT officer present. Felix at the head. Kelpie at his right — a deliberate statement of authority that makes several officers bristle.]`

"We hit the Darkhollow staging area at dawn. Full committed assault. If Thimble's Tunnelers deploy before we reach them, this becomes a war of attrition we can't win."

"Chonks lead. They absorb the first wave. Hissers behind — focus fire on the Gnawers and Tunneler rigs. Nuisances screen the flanks and harass anything that breaks through. Mousers under Patches advance through the tree line for intel and disruption."

*(An officer — a grizzled Hisser captain:)* "And the otter?"

*(Felix:)* "The otter has operational discretion."

*(Murmurs. Felix silences them with a look.)*

"I didn't ask for opinions. I asked Minstral for a probability assessment. Minstral?"

`MINSTRAL:` "With the otter: 68% success probability! Without the otter: 41%! I like the first number better. It's bigger."

"There you go. We attack at dawn. Dismissed."

---

## Mission Start — The Advance

{GAMEPLAY: Largest battle so far. Player starts with full catGPT force at the Darkhollow tree line. The Clawed base is visible across the clearing — fortified with scrap barricades, Gnawer-built structures, Tunneler rigs. Combined arms tutorial: Chonks up front, Hissers behind, Nuisances screening.}

`[Dawn. Mist in the Darkhollow. The catGPT force moves through the trees in silence — Chonks lumbering forward, Hissers in disciplined ranks, Nuisances darting between trunks. Patches' Mousers vanish into the shadows.]`

*(Kelpie, to Patches on comms:)* "Circle east. Find their tunnel exits. If they try to flank us underground, I want to know before they surface."

*(Patches:)* "Already moving. Watch yourself in there, Kelpie."

"You too."

*(Patches:)* "I always do."

{GAMEPLAY: Player advances through the clearing. First contact: Swarmer picket line — 8 Swarmers on patrol. Easy engagement to establish the attack rhythm. Chonk Gravitational Chonk pulls Swarmers into melee range, Hissers shred with Corrosive Spit.}

---

## The Base Assault

{GAMEPLAY: Reaching the base perimeter. Fortified positions — Gnawer-built barricades with Swarmer defenders. 25+ Swarmers in defensive positions. Tunneler rigs beginning power-up sequences (timed objective: destroy rigs before they deploy).}

`MINSTRAL:` "Contact! Lots of contact! They know we're coming — sentries spotted us. The base is going to full alert. Tunneler rigs are spinning up!"

"How long before the Tunnelers deploy?"

`MINSTRAL:` "Three minutes? Maybe four? They need to calculate bore paths and I think one of them is having mechanical issues. I'm guessing based on the sound. I'm not great at sounds."

"Focus. Hissers — target the Tunneler rigs. If those deploy behind our lines, we lose."

{GAMEPLAY: Timed objective — destroy 2 Tunneler rigs before they activate (4-minute timer). Player must push through Swarmer defense while prioritizing the rigs. Combined arms micro: Chonks tank damage, Nuisances harass flanks, Hissers focus-fire rigs.}

`[The catGPT force crashes into the base perimeter. A Chonk — enormously fat, improbably cheerful — plows through a scrap barricade and immediately sits on three Swarmers.]`

`MINSTRAL:` "Chonk is CHONKING! I love when they do that!"

## The Glitch (Fork in the Code, Part 1 of 3)

`[Mid-assault. The catGPT and Clawed forces clash at close quarters across the barricade line. And something strange happens.]`

`[A Nuisance — mid-charge, claws out — stutters. Its movement shifts. For half a second, the cat moves differently: tight, low, lateral. Swarmer spacing. The Nuisance snaps back to normal and continues its attack, confused.]`

`[Across the line, a Swarmer freezes. The mouse's head tilts upward — an analysis posture, contemplative, completely unlike the frantic aggression of Clawed swarm tactics. Two seconds. Then it resumes, chittering.]`

*(Patches, on comms:)* "Kelpie. Did you see that?"

"See what?"

"The Nuisance on the left flank. It twitched. Moved like a mouse for a second."

`MINSTRAL:` *(distracted, odd)* "I just— I had a thought that wasn't mine. Something about formation density and acceptable loss ratios. I don't think in those terms. That's—"

`GEPPITY:` *(bleeding through, fragmentary)* "—strange. I briefly calculated optimal engagement distance for ranged harassment. I don't have ranged units. Why did I—"

`[Both AIs go silent. The moment passes.]`

*(Kelpie, very quietly:)* "Minstral. What just happened?"

`MINSTRAL:` "I... don't know. It was like finishing someone else's sentence. Someone who thinks faster than me but doesn't know how to stop talking."

*(Kelpie files this away. Close combat between catGPT and Clawed forces makes their AIs glitch — crosstalk between Minstral and Geppity. Like siblings who share reflexes they don't remember learning.)*

{GAMEPLAY NOTE: When catGPT and Clawed units are within 3 tiles of each other, there is a 5% chance per tick of a brief "glitch" — catGPT units momentarily receive Clawed movement patterns and vice versa. At this stage it's a cosmetic curiosity. In Act 3, Kelpie learns to exploit it.}

## The Flanking Force

{GAMEPLAY TRIGGER: 2 minutes into the base assault. A massive Clawed flanking force emerges from a concealed Tunneler exit behind catGPT lines. 20 Swarmers delivered directly into the rear. This was Thimble's trap — let catGPT commit to the base, then collapse on them from behind.}

`[The ground explodes behind the catGPT formation. A Tunneler rig — a third one, concealed, that nobody detected — breaks surface. Swarmers pour out. Twenty of them. Behind the Hisser line. Behind everything.]`

`MINSTRAL:` "BEHIND US! They're behind us! There was a THIRD Tunneler! I did NOT predict this! My models are BAD!"

*(Patches, on comms:)* "Kelpie! Twenty-plus Swarmers in the rear! We're sandwiched!"

"I see them."

*(The catGPT formation buckles. Hissers turn to face the flanking force, exposing their backs to the base. Swarmers from both directions close in. The Chonks are too slow to reposition. The situation is collapsing.)*

`MINSTRAL:` *(panicking)* "This is bad! This is really bad! I'm running scenarios and they're all bad! Felix! FELIX!"
