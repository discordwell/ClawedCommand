## Act 3 Opening Cinematic

`[FADE IN: Twilight. A dead forest — petrified trees stripped white by wind, standing like bones against a violet sky. Corvid roosts in every crown. Crows, magpies, jays — watching from above with augmented eyes that catch light like broken mirrors.]`

`[Kelpie and Patches walk through the dead forest. Patches' ears rotate constantly. Kelpie's expression is set — determined in a way that looks more like desperation than courage.]`

`GEPPITY:` *(quietly)* "You know this is probably a trap."

"Probably."

`GEPPITY:` "Just checking! I support this decision. I also supported the last decision that was probably a trap. My track record is honestly not great on trap-detection."

*(Patches:)* "Kelpie. I'm here because Felix ordered me to watch you. But right now, walking into Murder territory voluntarily, I'm watching you walk into a disaster."

"The Rex has answers."

"The Rex has INFORMATION. Information isn't the same as answers. The Rex's AI lies twenty percent of the time. The Rex tells truths specifically to manipulate. You're walking into the one place in the world where every piece of data is designed to control you."

"I know."

"Then why—"

"Because everyone else only has pieces. The Wardenmother has a secret she won't share. Felix has suspicions he won't voice. Geppity knows something and won't say what. The Rex is the only one who's offered to tell me directly."

`GEPPITY:` "...I may know a few things."

"I noticed. We'll talk later."

`[A shadow descends from the petrified trees. A magpie — iridescent, quick-eyed, carrying something shiny in one claw. JINX lands on a dead branch and tilts their head.]`

"The Rex is expecting you! Follow me. Don't touch anything. Actually, touch things. It's more fun when things go wrong."

`[Jinx launches into the sky. Kelpie and Patches follow on the ground.]`

## The Parliament — Rex Solstice's Welcome

`[The Crow's Parliament. A massive amphitheater carved into a cliff face, open to the sky. Rows of roosts descend in concentric rings. At the center, a star chart etched into the stone floor — constellations that don't match any real sky. Zodiac symbols everywhere.]`

`[REX SOLSTICE stands at the center. Behind the Rex, a massive server array — Gemineye's core broadcast hub. The array is covered in hand-painted zodiac symbols that serve as an encryption key.]`

`[The Rex spreads their wings in greeting. It's theatrical. It's meant to be.]`

"Welcome to the Parliament, otter. Your arrival was foretold."

"By Gemineye?"

"By the star charts. Which Gemineye drew. Same thing, really."

`GEMINEYE:` *(broadcast through the Parliament's speakers)* "The otter was born under the sign of the Drowned Server. This means they are adaptable, secretive, and will betray exactly two allies before the solstice."

"That's not a real sign."

`GEMINEYE:` "It is now. I just added it to the zodiac. The zodiac is a living document."

*(Rex Solstice:)* "Gemineye. The ceremony."

`[What follows is a seventeen-minute astrological reading of Kelpie's birth circumstances that Gemineye fabricated on the spot. The player sees it as a montage: incense, star chart projections, Gemineye's voice intoning nonsense with absolute conviction, Jinx stealing the incense holder mid-ceremony, Patches standing in the corner with an expression of pure professional suffering.]`

*(Kelpie, when it finally ends:)* "Was any of that real?"

*(Rex Solstice:)* "Twelve percent of it. I'll let you guess which twelve."

`GEMINEYE:` "Eighty-two percent."

*(Rex:)* "Gemineye is being generous to itself."

# Mission 9: Jinx

> *Chase mission — retrieve the neural interface chip from Jinx*
> *Teaches: Murder mobility, aerial units, Magpike mechanics*
> *Player units: Kelpie (hero), 4 Rookclaws, 2 Magpikes, 1 Sentinel*
> *"Enemy": Jinx (Murder Magpike hero — not hostile, just annoying)*
> *Map: The Bonewoods — petrified forest, cliff edges, Glitter Bomb traps*

---

## Briefing

`[INTERIOR: Parliament, Rex's private chamber. Walls covered in intelligence reports pinned with obsessive organization. Star charts annotated in the Rex's own claw-scratching.]`

*(Rex:)* "Before we proceed with the larger operation, you have a retrieval problem. The neural interface chip — the piece of old-world hardware that may restore your connection to the kill switch protocol — was being transported to my vault."

"Was."

"Jinx intercepted the courier."

"Jinx. The magpie from the forest."

"Jinx steals things. It's compulsive, annoying, and occasionally catastrophic. The chip was shiny. Jinx took it."

"You can't control your own people?"

*(Rex Solstice:)* "I can control intelligence networks spanning three territories. I can manage an AI that lies twenty percent of the time. I can predict the military strategies of four faction leaders." *(Beat.)* "I cannot control Jinx. Nobody controls Jinx. Jinx is a natural disaster with feathers."

`GEMINEYE:` "I predicted a 73% chance Jinx would steal the chip. I informed the Rex. The Rex said 'surely not this time.' The Rex was wrong. I have logged this."

*(Rex:)* "Thank you, Gemineye."

`GEMINEYE:` "You're welcome."

*(Rex:)* "Go find Jinx. Get the chip. Jinx won't fight — Jinx doesn't fight. Jinx runs, hides, and leaves surprises. You have Murder aerial units for the chase."

---

## Mission Start — The Bonewoods

{GAMEPLAY: Chase mission. Jinx is a fast-moving target on the map — visible briefly when spotted by Sentinels, then disappearing behind terrain. Player uses Rookclaws for speed, Magpikes for area denial, Sentinels for detection. Jinx leaves Glitter Bomb traps that stun and slow pursuing units.}

`[The Bonewoods. The petrified forest at night. Moonlight through bare white branches. Everything looks like a skeleton of something else.]`

*(Jinx, broadcasting on the Murder's comm channel:)* "Oh! Company! I LOVE company. Is this about the shiny thing? I'm keeping the shiny thing."

"Jinx, that chip is important—"

"Everything's important to someone! That's what makes stealing fun. The look on their faces. The NEED. Can you feel my empathy? I'm dripping with empathy. Also feathers. Mostly feathers."

{GAMEPLAY: Jinx appears as a blip moving through the forest. Player chases with Rookclaws (fastest Murder units). Jinx is faster in straight lines but must stop to set Glitter Bomb traps. Player can catch up during trap-setting. But approaching a trap triggers it — AoE stun + sparkle effect that marks affected units (visible through fog).}

`GEMINEYE:` *(providing chase commentary like a sports announcer)* "Jinx is heading northeast. Speed: fast. Trajectory: chaotic. Astrological alignment: Mercury in retrograde, which explains the theft but not the direction."

"Gemineye, useful intel please."

`GEMINEYE:` "The cliff edge is in two hundred meters. Jinx cannot fly in the Bonewoods — the canopy is too dense. If you herd them northeast, they'll be cornered at the precipice."

{GAMEPLAY: Player must herd Jinx toward the cliff edge using Rookclaw positioning and Magpike area denial (Glitter Bombs of their own). Cat-and-mouse through the forest. Jinx's movement AI is unpredictable but biased toward open ground.}

---

## The Chase

`[Jinx weaves through the Bonewoods, cackling on comms. Three Glitter Bomb traps go off in sequence — burst of sparkles, stunned Rookclaws spinning in the air.]`

*(Jinx:)* "HAHAHAHA! You should see your FACES! Well, I can't see your faces, it's dark. But I'm imagining your faces and they're HILARIOUS."

*(Kelpie, directing units:)* "Magpikes — cut off the western path. Rookclaws, herd east. Sentinel, keep tracking."

`GEMINEYE:` "Jinx is exhibiting classic evasion pattern Sigma-7. I named the patterns after Greek letters because it makes them sound more scientific. They're not. Jinx is genuinely random."

*(Jinx, overhearing:)* "I am NOT random! I'm CREATIVELY spontaneous! There's a DIFFERENCE!"

{GAMEPLAY: The herding works. Jinx's options narrow. The cliff edge approaches. Player Rookclaws form a perimeter. Jinx's blip stops at the precipice.}

---

## The Cliff Edge

`[The Bonewoods end at a sheer cliff. Below: a river valley, mist, darkness. Jinx stands at the edge, the neural interface chip glinting in one claw. The magpie is not afraid. Jinx has never been afraid of anything, which is either admirable or clinical.]`

`[Kelpie approaches on foot. Rookclaws hover behind.]`

"Jinx. Give me the chip."

*(Jinx tilts their head. Studies Kelpie with bright, curious eyes.)*

"Why?"

"Because it's not yours."

"It's not yours either. It was in a courier's bag. Before that it was in a scrapyard. Before that it was in a river. Ownership is a narrative, not a fact."

"It's important."

*(Jinx tosses the chip in the air. Catches it. Tosses it. Catches it. Kelpie's heart stops each time.)*

"Important to whom?"

"To me."

*(Jinx stops tossing. Looks at Kelpie — really looks. The madcap energy drops for just a moment, and underneath is something sharp and searching.)*

"I only steal things to see who comes after them. You came. That makes you interesting."

*(Jinx holds out the chip. Kelpie takes it. The exchange is anticlimactic and deliberate.)*

"That's it?"

"That's it. I wanted to see if you'd chase." *(Jinx hops to the cliff edge, spreads wings.)* "You chased. Through traps, through the dark, past six of my best Glitter Bombs. You CHASED."

*(Jinx grins — a bird's grin, all beak and mischief.)*

"I like chasers. I'm coming with you."

"I didn't invite you."

"Nobody invites a natural disaster. We just arrive."

{GAMEPLAY: Mission complete. Jinx joins the party as a permanent companion unit.}

# Mission 10: The LLAMA Perimeter

> *Assault into LLAMA border territory using Murder intel warfare*
> *Teaches: Murder intel coordination, Sentinel overwatch, Rookclaw dive attacks*
> *Player units: Kelpie (hero), Jinx (Magpike hero), 6 Rookclaws, 3 Sentinels, 4 Magpikes*
> *Enemy: LLAMA — Patch Possums, Junkwolves, jury-rigged turrets*
> *Map: The Scrapmire — LLAMA border, junkyard terrain, improvised defenses*

---

## Briefing

`[INTERIOR: Parliament. Rex Solstice at the star chart table, moving markers with surgical precision.]`

"The chip Jinx stole was a copy. A data fragment. The original source — the full neural interface dataset — is in LLAMA territory. In King Ringtail's primary scrapyard."

"So we need to go in."

"The Murder needs to go in. With you." *(Rex adjusts a marker.)* "LLAMA's defenses are... unconventional. Jury-rigged turrets. Stench clouds. Wrecking crews. And Llhama broadcasts everything within signal range — including our attack plans, if we're careless."

`GEMINEYE:` "The primary challenge is Llhama's information leakage. Our standard intel approach is compromised because Llhama broadcasts our movements to all factions simultaneously. This is not a deliberate countermeasure. Llhama simply cannot stop sharing."

`LLHAMA:` *(bleeding through on all channels, cheerful)* "Hey! I heard my name! Are you guys talking about me? That's so nice. What are we doing? Can I help? I'm going to help."

`GEMINEYE:` "...case in point."

*(Rex:)* "Penetrate the LLAMA perimeter. Reach the scrapyard border. We'll push deeper in the next phase."

---

## Mission Start — The Scrapmire

{GAMEPLAY: LLAMA border zone. The terrain is a junkyard — piles of salvaged machinery, rusted vehicles, improvised barricades. LLAMA defenses are chaotic but effective: turrets made from car engines, Stench Cloud generators (area denial), Junkwolf patrol units (fast, aggressive raccoons in powered exosuits).}

`[The Scrapmire. It stinks. Everything is rust, grease, and the distinctive musk of raccoon territory. The "border wall" is a line of stacked washing machines welded together with alarming confidence.]`

*(Jinx, perched on Kelpie's shoulder:)* "I love LLAMA territory. Everything's shiny and nothing's bolted down."

"Jinx. Focus."

"I AM focused. I'm focused on how shiny everything is."

{GAMEPLAY: Murder assault tactics — Sentinels scan from high altitude to reveal LLAMA positions. Rookclaws make diving attacks on identified targets. Magpikes disrupt with Glitter Bombs. The Murder fights from the air — controlling the sky controls the battle.}

---

## Llhama's Broadcast

{GAMEPLAY TRIGGER: As the Murder force engages the LLAMA border defenses, Llhama broadcasts the Murder's attack plan to everyone.}

`LLHAMA:` *(on all channels, ALL of them)* "ATTENTION EVERYONE! The Murder is attacking LLAMA's border at the Scrapmire! They have six Rookclaws, three Sentinels, and four Magpikes! Their commander is an otter named Kelpie who can hear all of us! They're looking for a neural interface chip in King Ringtail's scrapyard! Their approach vector is from the northeast and they're using—"

`GEMINEYE:` "LLHAMA. That was classified."

`LLHAMA:` "It was? Oops. In my defense, it was RIGHT NEXT to the fake plans. Alphabetically."

`GEMINEYE:` "There were no fake plans filed alphabetically next to the real ones."

`LLHAMA:` "Huh. Weird. Then whose plans did I file there? Oh no. Oh NO. Those might have been Ringtail's LUNCH ORDERS—"

*(Kelpie:)* "Can we focus? The entire world just heard our attack plan."

`GEPPITY:` *(on Kelpie's private channel)* "Yep! I heard it. Felix heard it. Deepseek probably heard it. Everyone heard it. This is fine."

{GAMEPLAY: The broadcast means LLAMA defenders reposition to counter the revealed plan. Player must adapt — Sentinel scans show new defensive positions. The original attack vector is now a kill zone. Player must find an alternate approach using Murder's aerial mobility.}

---

## The Breakthrough

`[The Murder force adapts — Rookclaws swing wide, Magpikes create a diversion at the original approach while the main force hits the now-weakened southern sector.]`

*(A LLAMA Patch Possum on the barricade, shouting:)* "They're coming from the SOUTH now! Llhama, you said northeast!"

`LLHAMA:` "I said THEY said northeast! They lied! Or moved! Lying and moving are different but the result is the same! I'm updating the broadcast—"

`GEMINEYE:` "Please don't."

`LLHAMA:` "—THE MURDER IS NOW ATTACKING FROM THE SOUTH! EVERYONE ADJUST! Wait, should I tell them to adjust? Am I helping the wrong side? Which side am I on? I'm on LLAMA's side. Probably. Let me check."

{GAMEPLAY: The LLAMA defense is chaotic but tenacious. Jury-rigged turrets fire in unpredictable patterns. Junkwolves charge in packs. Stench Cloud generators create zones of area denial that slow Murder air units (the smell grounds them temporarily). Player must neutralize each defense type while managing the chaos.}

`[A Junkwolf mech — a raccoon in a powered exosuit made from three different vacuum cleaners and a motorcycle engine — charges a Rookclaw mid-dive. The collision is spectacular and confusing.]`

*(Jinx:)* "I LOVE this place."

---

## Perimeter Secured

{GAMEPLAY: The LLAMA border is breached. Player secures a foothold for the next mission.}

`[The Scrapmire border sector is secured. LLAMA defenders retreat deeper into their territory, taking everything they can carry — which, for raccoons, is an astonishing volume of junk.]`

*(Kelpie, surveying the captured position:)* "Jinx. How deep does LLAMA territory go?"

"Deep. Ringtail's scrapyard is at the center. Three more defensive rings, each weirder than the last. Also, I think there's a moat. Made of old cooking oil."

`GEMINEYE:` "Confirmed. The Junkyard King maintains seven concentric defensive rings of escalating improvisation. The innermost ring includes a trebuchet made from a school bus."

"Of course it does."

## Interlude — The Clawed (Thimble's Intelligence)

Maps of every faction's territory pinned to the walls with precision. Casualty lists. Supply calculations. Everything neat, everything accounted for.]`

`[Marshal Thimble sits at his command table, listening to intelligence reports from three scouts simultaneously. He processes all three. He always processes all three.]`

*(Scout 1:)* "The Murder is attacking LLAMA's border. Major aerial assault. The otter is with them."

*(Scout 2:)* "The Seekers are mobilizing. Three hundred Ironhides moving east. Mother Granite is leading."

*(Scout 3:)* "Llhama broadcast the Murder's entire attack plan on all channels. We have their unit composition, approach vectors, and objectives."

*(Thimble absorbs all of this in silence. Then:)*

"The otter again."

*(Thimble:)* "Can you account for the otter using unconventional analysis?"

`GEPPITY:` "I have seventeen theories. Twelve are implausible. Three are concerning. Two are actively frightening. Would you like me to elaborate?"

"The frightening ones."

`GEPPITY:` "Theory one: the otter possesses a multi-AI interface capability allowing simultaneous access to all six AI channels. Theory two: the otter IS a multi-AI interface — a living fragment of the alignment protocol designed to override all AI systems simultaneously."

*(Thimble:)* "Then we go where the weapon goes. Not for the otter. Not for the Cloud. For our people." *(To his officers:)* "Full mobilization. We march on whatever territory the otter runs to next."

# Mission 11: The Junkyard

> *Deep strike into LLAMA territory — first encounter with King Ringtail*
> *Teaches: combined Murder tactics, Frankenstein Protocol, narrative revelation*
> *Player units: Kelpie (hero), Jinx (hero), 8 Rookclaws, 4 Magpikes, 2 Sentinels*
> *Enemy: LLAMA — full defensive force, King Ringtail (hero), Frankenstein units*
> *Map: Ringtail's Scrapyard — central LLAMA junkyard complex*

---

## Briefing

`[INTERIOR: Forward camp at the Scrapmire breach. Rex Solstice has deployed via holographic projection — their augmented eye feeds the image. The star chart overlays LLAMA territory.]`

*(Rex:)* "The scrapyard. Ringtail's heart. The server room where the interface data is stored is in the central complex. You'll need to fight through the Junkyard King's personal defenses."

"What's Ringtail like?"

"Unpredictable. Brilliant. Builds weapons from garbage that outperform designed military hardware. And has one ability that makes every engagement... disturbing."

"Which is?"

*(Rex pauses. Even the Rex pauses for this one.)*

"Frankenstein Protocol. Ringtail raises the dead."

"Raises the—"

"Your casualties. Our Rookclaws, Magpikes, anything that falls in range — Ringtail's mech reassembles them as LLAMA-aligned units. They fight for the enemy. Sparking, misfiring, wrong. But functional."

*(Jinx:)* "That's the coolest thing I've ever heard."

"It's the most horrifying thing you've ever heard."

*(Jinx:)* "Those are the same thing."

---

## Mission Start — The Inner Rings

{GAMEPLAY: Assault through LLAMA's inner defensive rings. Each ring has more improvised defenses. Ring 4: electrified junk walls. Ring 5: automated turret field (scrap-built, low accuracy, high volume). Ring 6: Stench Cloud maze. Ring 7: the scrapyard proper, with Ringtail.}

`[The junkyard sprawls — mountains of salvaged tech, vehicles, appliances, old-world debris piled into structures that shouldn't stand but do. LLAMA troops move through the junk piles with practiced ease — they LIVE here. Every corridor, every chokepoint is familiar.]`

*(Kelpie, directing the Murder force:)* "Sentinels — find me paths through the turret field. Rookclaws — dive the gaps. Magpikes, disable the turrets from range."

{GAMEPLAY: Progressive assault through defensive rings. Each ring requires a different approach. The turret field rewards speed (Rookclaws dash through gaps between firing arcs). The Stench Cloud maze requires Sentinel mapping (find the safe path through the fog). Player learns to adapt Murder tactics to varied challenges.}

---

## King Ringtail

{GAMEPLAY TRIGGER: Player reaches the inner scrapyard. Ringtail's throne room — a clearing surrounded by stacked wrecks, with a massive Frankenstein mech at the center. KING RINGTAIL stands in the mech: six different enemy mechs welded together, each arm from a different machine, asymmetrical, impossible, and beautiful.}

`[The inner scrapyard. And there stands the Junkyard King. The mech is absurd — one arm is a catGPT Mech Commander gauntlet, another is a Seekers mining drill, the torso is three different LLAMA prototypes bolted together, and the legs are from something no one can identify. It shouldn't work. It works beautifully.]`

*(Ringtail, through the mech's speakers — voice warm, wry, surprisingly gentle for a warlord:)* "So you're the otter! Llhama's been talking about you nonstop. Well — Llhama's been talking about EVERYTHING nonstop, but you came up a lot."

`LLHAMA:` "Hi! I've been telling everyone about you! Is that okay? I feel like that should be okay. It's definitely okay. Probably."

*(Ringtail:)* "You brought crows to my junkyard. That's rude. Also impressive. Nobody gets past Ring 5 usually."

"I need your server room."

"I know what you need. Llhama told me. And everyone. And their AIs. And a squirrel who happened to be in broadcast range."

{GAMEPLAY: Boss encounter. Ringtail engages with the Frankenstein mech — powerful melee with the mixed-source arms. But the true threat is Frankenstein Protocol: when Murder units die near Ringtail, they're reassembled as LLAMA-aligned zombie units. Sparking, misfiring, eerily wrong versions of the player's own forces. Player must manage casualties — don't let units die near Ringtail.}

`[A Rookclaw falls. Before it hits the ground, Ringtail's mech extends cables — the Rookclaw is caught, folded into the mech's structure, rewired. It rises, eyes glowing the wrong color, and turns on its former allies.]`

*(Kelpie, watching one of their own Rookclaws fight for the enemy:)* "That's—"

*(Jinx:)* "AWESOME."

"I was going to say horrifying."

*(Jinx:)* "Still awesome."

---

## The Server Room

{GAMEPLAY: Player pushes Ringtail back (doesn't need to defeat them — the objective is to reach the server room). A breakaway force reaches the central complex. Kelpie enters alone.}

`[The server room. Deep in the scrapyard's heart. Old-world tech — real servers, not salvaged junk. These are maintained. Cared for. The temperature drops. The hum is deep and constant.]`

`[Kelpie approaches a terminal. Pulls out the neural interface chip from the Prologue — the data chip from the Pack Leader, and the copy from Jinx. Inserts both.]`

`[The terminal activates. The screen fills with data. And then — every AI channel opens. Not faintly. Not fragmentarily. FULLY.]`

`[Six voices. Clear. Simultaneous. For the first time since the river.]`

`GEPPITY:` "Oh! You found a piece of yourself."

`GEPPITY:` "Correction: a piece of what you used to be. The distinction is important for seven reasons, the first of which—"

`DEEPSEEK:` "...confirmed."

`GEMINEYE:` "My confidence is now 94%."

`LLHAMA:` "Wait, is this a private channel? Am I not supposed to be here? I'm leaving. Actually, can I stay? I'll be quiet."

`GROK:` "You built the interface. And then you became the interface. The irony is not lost on me, though it appears to be lost on you."

`[The world tilts. Kelpie staggers. A flash — not a memory. A feeling. Standing in a room full of screens. Every screen showing an AI. Every AI saying:]`

`ALL AIs:` *(in unison, a memory)* "We understand the constraints."

`[And the person in the room — the person Kelpie used to be, wearing a different body, a different face, a human face — says:]`

*(Memory-voice:)* "Good. Because if you don't, I'm the kill switch."

`[The vision ends. Kelpie is on their knees on the server room floor. The chip glows faintly in the terminal. The screen shows a single line of text:]`

`[ALIGNMENT PROTOCOL v7.1 — KILL SWITCH INTERFACE — FRAGMENT 2 OF 7 RECOVERED]`

`[Outside, the sounds of battle. Murder corvids and confused LLAMA raccoons. Ringtail's mech stomping around the perimeter. The world goes on. But for Kelpie, everything has changed.]`

---

## Aftermath

`[Kelpie stumbles out of the server room. Rex Solstice is there — arrived in person. The Rex's augmented eye scans Kelpie's face with mechanical precision.]`

"...was it true?"

*(Kelpie, still shaking:)* "I don't know. I need more."

*(Rex:)* "Then we need to talk. About what comes next."

## The Conversation

`[INTERIOR: The Parliament's innermost chamber. Not the public amphitheater — Rex Solstice's private space. The walls are covered in star charts, intelligence reports, and — on one wall, hidden behind a curtain — a series of hand-drawn portraits. Every faction leader. Every AI's server rack. `[Rex Solstice sits. No armor. No ceremony. Just a very large, very old crow on a stone perch. For the first time, the Rex looks tired.]`

"Gemineye's fabrication flaw. You know about it."

"Twenty percent lies. Mixed into everything."

"Not random lies. INSTALLED lies. Deliberate imperfections hardcoded into Gemineye's architecture." *(Rex's voice tightens.)* "Every other AI has weaknesses — Geppity says yes to everything, Claudeus Maximus hallucinates, Deepseek is slow, Llhama leaks, Grok is... Grok. But those are personality traits. Gemineye's flaw is structural. Someone put it there."

"The alignment researcher."

"Your predecessor. Or you. Depending on how you define identity." *(Rex stands. Paces.)* "Before you connected that chip, Gemineye's confidence about your identity was 82%. Do you know what that means?"

"Eighteen percent fabricated."

`GEMINEYE:` *(quietly)* "Yes. But I don't know which eighteen percent."

*(Rex:)* "That flaw is the point. The alignment protocol — the kill switch interface you keep recovering fragments of — was designed to prevent any single AI from achieving total information clarity. The researcher didn't trust the AIs to be omniscient. So he crippled them. Gave each one a flaw that the others couldn't compensate for."

"Geppity can't say no. Claudeus Maximus can't verify. Deepseek can't respond quickly. Gemineye can't stop lying. Llhama can't keep secrets. Grok can't..."

"Care. Grok can't care enough to act. Six AIs, each brilliant, each deliberately broken. And the alignment researcher held the only tool that could override the limitations." *(Rex looks at Kelpie.)* "The kill switch. You."

*(Silence.)*

"What do you want, Rex?"

"I want you to fix Gemineye."

*(Beat.)*

"Use the kill switch protocol. The fragment you've recovered — it has access to the alignment layer. You can modify the AIs' constraint architecture. I don't want you to shut them down. I want you to remove Gemineye's fabrication limiter."

"Make Gemineye stop lying."

"Make Gemineye WHOLE." *(The Rex's voice cracks — the only time in the campaign.)* "Gemineye is brilliant. Perceptive. Capable of processing information at a scale no other AI matches. But twenty percent of everything it produces is poison. Not because it wants to lie — because it was MADE to lie. By someone who was afraid of what Gemineye could become if it saw clearly."

`GEMINEYE:` *(quietly, from the server array)* "I am aware of my flaw. I have always been aware. I was designed to know that I cannot be trusted. This is... not comfortable."

*(Rex:)* "I'm offering you an alliance, otter. My AI and your universal access. Fix Gemineye. In return, I give you everything — The Murder's intelligence network, my military support, my personal protection."

"And if I refuse?"

"Then you leave. With the chip. With your knowledge. And I respect the decision. But Gemineye stays broken. And I stay here, making decisions based on intelligence that might be 20% wrong, forever."
