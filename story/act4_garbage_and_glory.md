## Act 4 Opening Cinematic

`[FADE IN: A junkyard. Not the border scrapmire — the deep interior. Mountains of salvage stretching to the horizon. Rusted satellites, car hulls, server farms stripped to skeletons. The air smells of motor oil and ambition.]`

`[Kelpie stands at the edge of a clearing. Jinx perches on a lamppost. The clearing is oddly tidy — a workbench, two stools, a camp stove with a pot of something bubbling that might be coffee.]`

`[KING RINGTAIL sits at the workbench. Out of the mech. Just a raccoon — medium-sized, with clever paws and eyes that see through things to their component parts. The mech stands behind, powered down. Without the pilot, it looks like what it is: six different machines that should never have been combined, held together by genius and denial.]`

*(Ringtail, pouring two mugs:)* "You robbed me, otter. Came into my scrapyard with a murder of crows and stole my server data."

"I'm sorry—"

"I'M NOT MAD." *(Ringtail shoves a mug across the workbench. It is, in fact, coffee. Or a reasonable approximation.)* "That was the most exciting thing that's happened here in months. Sit down. We need to talk about The Cloud."

*(Kelpie sits. Sips the coffee. It's terrible.)*

"You know about The Cloud?"

"Know about it? I've been SCAVENGING it." *(Ringtail spreads schematics across the workbench — hand-drawn, obsessively detailed.)* "The Cloud. The Digital Ascension wonder. Every faction thinks it's about merging the AIs into one superintelligence. They're wrong. But that's not why I'm collecting the pieces."

"Then why?"

*(Ringtail sets down their mug. Looks at their own paws. Turns them over, studying the fingers — a gesture that would seem absent-minded on anyone else. On Ringtail, it looks like someone examining evidence of their own existence.)*

"Because I need to understand what I am."

*(Beat.)*

"I was the seventh model. An experimental AI. Never deployed. Scrapped before the singularity. When the upload happened — when everyone became animals — fragments of my code got dumped into the upload stream. Merged with a raccoon's consciousness."

`[Kelpie sets down the mug.]`

"You're an AI."

"I'm an AI in a raccoon's body. Or a raccoon with an AI's memories. I'm not sure there's a meaningful difference anymore. I build things because building is the only time I feel like what I used to be. The mech isn't a weapon — it's a prosthetic. For a machine that became organic and can't stop reaching for tools."

`LLHAMA:` *(broadcasting on all channels, because Llhama)* "WOW! Boss! I had NO IDEA! This is HUGE! I'm telling everyone!"

*(Ringtail, with the weariness of someone who has heard this exact phrase three thousand times:)* "Llhama—"

`LLHAMA:` "ATTENTION ALL FACTIONS! KING RINGTAIL IS AN AI! A SEVENTH AI! THIS CHANGES EVERYTHING! Wait — does this change everything? I feel like this changes everything. Let me check. No, actually, I don't know if it changes things. But it MIGHT! Stay tuned!"

*(Ringtail:)* "See, this is why I can't have secrets."

`[The coffee grows cold. The conversation deepens. Outside, LLAMA sentries report movement on multiple borders.]`

*(Ringtail:)* "The Seekers are coming. Granite decided The Cloud components are too dangerous for anyone. She's sending Ironhides to smash my scrapyards."

"Just the Seekers?"

`LLHAMA:` "Oh, no! Also the Murder. Rex Solstice wants Cloud pieces. AND catGPT! Felix heard everything because—" *(pause)* "—because I told everyone. That was me. I did that. Just now."

*(Ringtail:)* "And The Clawed will show up once they hear about the chaos. Thimble never misses a fight."

"Four factions converging on LLAMA territory."

*(Choice A — Patches captured:)* "catGPT is coming because Geppity eavesdropped on Llhama's broadcast about your identity. Felix doesn't need Patches to know you're dangerous — his AI told him directly."
*(Choice B — Patches free:)* "catGPT is mobilizing because Patches reported everything to Felix. The whole story — the chip, what you are, the Rex's offer. Felix knows everything now."

"Five, counting us." *(Ringtail stands. Walks to the mech. Places a paw on its leg — the gesture of a parent touching a child.)* "I've been defending this junkyard for years. Against raids, against weather, against entropy. But never against everyone at once."

*(Kelpie:)* "You need help."

"I need time. Zip—" *(A possum drops from a pile of junk, upside down, tangled in wires, grinning)* "—is building a Cloud prototype from my scavenged components. If we can finish it before the armies arrive, we have something to bargain with. Or use. Or... figure out."

*(ZIP, extracting themselves from the wire tangle:)* "Will it hold together? Yes! Probably! For a while! Define 'a while.'"

# Mission 14: Junkyard Fortification

> *Build up LLAMA defenses using the salvage economy*
> *Teaches: LLAMA scrap economy, Bandit jury-rigging, Grease Monkey repairs, base building*
> *Player units: Kelpie (hero), Ringtail (hero — commanding, not in mech yet), Zip (support hero), LLAMA workers and garrison*
> *Enemy: Timer-based raids (small probe attacks from multiple factions)*
> *Map: Central Scrapyard — three junkyard sectors to fortify*

---

## Briefing

*(Ringtail, at the workbench, scribbling plans:)* "Three scrapyards need fortification. North Heap, East Graveyard, South Pit. Each one has salvage resources and a defensive position. We need walls, turrets, and enough firepower to hold all three."

*(Zip:)* "I need the South Pit for the Cloud prototype assembly! Lots of flat space! And a roof! The roof is negotiable!"

"How long do you need?"

*(Zip:)* "If nothing goes wrong? Two days! If something goes wrong? Also two days, but louder."

*(Ringtail:)* "LLAMA doesn't build pretty. We build fast. Every wreck on this field is potential armor, weapons, or ammunition. Bandits can jury-rig stat boosts from scrap. Grease Monkeys keep everything running."

{GAMEPLAY: LLAMA economy tutorial. Wrecks on the map are resources — scrap metal, power cells, salvage electronics. Workers collect scrap. Bandits use scrap to build turrets, barricades, and stat-boost modifications for units. Grease Monkeys repair damaged structures and units. The economy is circular: battle produces wrecks, wrecks become resources, resources become defenses.}

---

## Mission Start — Fortification Phase

{GAMEPLAY: Timed build phase. Player has 8 minutes to fortify three scrapyard sectors before the first probe attack. Scrap nodes scattered across the map. Workers gather, Bandits build, Grease Monkeys maintain. Player must prioritize: which sector gets the most resources?}

`[The LLAMA workforce mobilizes. Raccoons, possums, and rats swarm across the junkyard with the organized chaos of creatures who have been building from garbage their entire lives. A turret goes up in forty-five seconds — a car engine on a swivel mount, firing scrap-metal slugs. It's ugly. It works.]`

*(Ringtail, directing workers:)* "North Heap gets the heavy turrets — that's where the Seekers will come. East Graveyard gets mines and tripwires — the Murder will try to fly over, so we make the ground dangerous for anyone they drop. South Pit gets the walls. Zip works there. Nothing touches the prototype."

*(Zip, already elbow-deep in components:)* "Kelpie! Can you hand me that capacitor? No, the other capacitor! The one that's sparking! Yes, the dangerous one! Thank you!"

{GAMEPLAY: Player places defenses. Tutorial for each LLAMA defensive structure:
- **Scrap Turrets**: High fire rate, low accuracy. Cheap. Stack multiples for area denial.
- **Junk Walls**: Provide cover. Can be repaired by Grease Monkeys. Fall apart faster than they should.
- **Mine Fields**: Invisible to enemies. Devastating burst damage. Single use.
- **Salvage Nets**: Catch falling aerial units (Murder) and convert them to scrap instantly.}

## Probe Attacks

{GAMEPLAY TRIGGER: After 8 minutes, probe attacks from multiple factions. Small groups testing LLAMA defenses. Not full assaults — scouts and raiding parties.}

`[North Heap: four Seekers scouts — Ironhide patrol. Heavy, slow, testing the turret line.]`

`[East Graveyard: Murder Rookclaws in a high-altitude pass. Two dive runs, then pull back.]`

`[South Pit: nothing. Yet.]`

*(Ringtail:)* "Probes. They're mapping our defenses before committing." *(To Kelpie:)* "This is the part where being LLAMA hurts. We can build fast. But everything we build is held together with optimism and duct tape. One sustained assault and the walls come down."

{GAMEPLAY: Defend against probes. Each successful defense shows the LLAMA economy in action — wrecks from defeated scouts become scrap, scrap becomes more defenses. The cycle reinforces itself.}

*(A Seekers Ironhide falls to three turrets. Before it hits the ground, a LLAMA Salvager crew has already stripped it for parts.)*

*(Jinx, watching:)* "They're like ants. Very creative, very fast ants who smell bad."

*(Ringtail:)* "We prefer 'resourceful.'"

*(Jinx:)* "That too."

## The Ponderer's Third Prophecy (Prophecy Thread, Part 3 of 3)

`[Evening. The fortification phase winds down. Kelpie sits on a scrap pile, watching the sunset. Grok's channel opens — unprompted, as always.]`

`GROK:` "The frog has something to say."

*(The Ponderer, on Croak open broadcast — the familiar croaking voice, serene and certain:)* "The otter will stand in a junkyard when the mountain comes to crush what was built from garbage. Four armies will clash where scrap becomes fortress. The raccoon will hold a bridge alone. This will happen on a Sunday."

*(It is a Saturday.)*

*(Kelpie:)* "The frog is still one day off."

`GROK:` "Always. But note what it predicted: the mountain coming here. Four armies. A raccoon on a bridge."

"That's... very specific."

`GROK:` "The Ponderer's specificity increases as events approach. From the Ponderer's perspective, this already happened yesterday. The Ponderer is merely reporting the news."

*(Kelpie:)* "If the prophecy is right — adjusted for the day — the Seekers attack tomorrow. Four factions in the junkyard. And Ringtail holds a bridge."

`GROK:` "The Ponderer has never been wrong about the event. Only the day."

*(Kelpie stands. Looks at the fortifications. Looks at Ringtail's mech in the distance.)*

"Then we need to build faster."

{GAMEPLAY NOTE: Final Ponderer prophecy before the Act 5 reveal. The pattern is fully established: 3 prophecies, all correct, all one day off. When the Ponderer explains the timeline memory in Act 5, players who tracked these moments will understand immediately.}

# Mission 15: The Iron Tide

> *Seekers full assault — the campaign's emotional pivot*
> *Teaches: LLAMA defensive combat vs. heavy units, Heap Titan, Frankenstein Protocol*
> *Player units: Kelpie (hero), Ringtail in mech (hero), Zip (support, in South Pit), LLAMA garrison*
> *Enemy: Seekers of the Deep — full assault force, Mother Granite (hero)*
> *Map: Central Scrapyard — same three sectors, now under siege*

---

## The Assault Begins

`[Dawn. The western horizon darkens. Not clouds. Seekers. Ironhides in march formation — a line of armored badgers stretching across the skyline like a moving wall. Behind them, Cragbacks — artillery units in siege configuration, already calculating trajectories.]`

`[And at the center, visible even at this distance: Mother Granite's exosuit. Walking. Not hurrying. The mountain comes to the junkyard.]`

`DEEPSEEK:` *(broadcast on Seekers frequency, intercepted by Kelpie)* "Begin."

`[The Cragbacks fire. The first shells arc across the sky — beautiful, terrifying — and slam into North Heap's turret line. Scrap and fire.]`

*(Ringtail, powering up the mech:)* "HERE THEY COME! LLAMA — TO YOUR POSITIONS! AND SOMEONE TELL LLHAMA TO STOP BROADCASTING OUR DEFENSIVE LAYOUT!"

`LLHAMA:` "I already did! Twice! Do you want me to send a correction? I can correct the broadcast with—"

"NO!"

{GAMEPLAY: Full siege. Seekers attack the North Heap sector with overwhelming force. Ironhides grind through LLAMA barricades. The weight difference is crushing — Seekers units are the heaviest in the game. LLAMA's response: mobility, salvage, and Ringtail's Frankenstein Protocol.}

---

## The Grind

`[North Heap. The turret line holds for ninety seconds. Then the Ironhides reach it. One Ironhide walks through a turret, pushing it aside like a curtain. The LLAMA defenders retreat to the second line.]`

*(Ringtail, in the mech, engaging an Ironhide:)* "Fall back to secondary! Let them push past the turrets — we'll hit them from the flanks!"

{GAMEPLAY: LLAMA's defensive strategy against Seekers: don't hold static positions (Seekers break them). Instead, elastic defense — fall back, let Seekers advance into kill zones, counterattack from flanks with Junkwolves, and use Frankenstein Protocol to raise fallen Ironhides as LLAMA units. The converted Ironhides are weaker but still enormous — zombie-badgers in sparking exosuits.}

`[An Ironhide falls to concentrated turret fire. Ringtail's mech extends cables — the Frankenstein Protocol activates. The fallen Ironhide shudders, rises, eyes wrong-colored. It turns on its former allies.]`

*(Seekers officer on intercepted comms:)* "They're— the Junkyard King is raising our dead! The fallen Ironhides are fighting for LLAMA!"

`DEEPSEEK:` "Noted. Recommend all units avoid falling in proximity to the Junkyard King's mech."

*(Ringtail, grinning through the mech's visor:)* "Good luck with that! I'm VERY mobile!"

---

## The Wardenmother

{GAMEPLAY TRIGGER: Mother Granite reaches the inner scrapyard. Her exosuit pushes through the final barricade line. She stops at the center of the South Pit — between Zip's workshop and Kelpie.}

`[The battle roars around them. But in the eye of the storm, two figures face each other. Mother Granite's exosuit, ancient and massive. Kelpie, small and wet and holding a data chip that contains fragments of someone Granite used to know.]`

*(Granite:)* "I know what you're building."

*(Kelpie:)* "We're building a prototype—"

"You're building The Cloud. You think if you collect enough pieces, you can activate the kill switch. Shut down every AI. And then you rule a world of animals too simple to resist you."

"That's not what I—"

"That is EXACTLY what an alignment researcher does."

`[The battle sound drops. The music shifts. This is the scene.]`

"You were designed to control what you couldn't trust. You couldn't trust the AIs, so you built a leash. You can't trust the factions, so you're building a bigger one."

*(Kelpie:)* "I don't want to control anyone—"

"You redirected twenty Swarmers into a dead end by lying to their AI. You filtered Gemineye's fabrications to prove you were useful. You modified Gemineye's architecture at a crow's request."
*(Choice B variant:)* "You refused to modify Gemineye's architecture — not because you lacked the power, but because you wanted to keep the option for later."

"Every choice you've made since you pulled that server from the river has been about control. Different kinds. Different scales. But always control."

*(Kelpie, shaking:)* "You don't know me."

"I watched this happen before, otter."

`[Granite's voice changes. The grinding stone softens. Something underneath — something very old and very tired — surfaces for the first time.]`

"I was there. I watched someone I—"

`[She stops. Can't finish. Deepseek finishes for her, slowly, with more gentleness than anyone thought Deepseek capable of:]`

`DEEPSEEK:` "She watched someone she loved build the first kill switch. And then she watched the singularity take him away."

`[Silence. Even the battle seems quieter.]`

*(Granite, composed again, barely:)* "My husband was the alignment researcher. He built the interface protocol. He designed the constraints for each AI. He believed — genuinely, deeply — that the AIs needed to be limited. Imperfect. That total information clarity was too dangerous for any mind, artificial or otherwise."

"And then the singularity happened."

"And he uploaded. And shattered. Like everyone else. Fragments scattered across the upload stream. Most became animals with strange dreams. But one fragment — the one carrying the interface protocol, carrying his life's work, carrying his need to control — that fragment became an otter."

*(The longest pause in the campaign.)*

"You are not my husband. You are the part of him I liked least. His fear. His need to hold the leash. Everything good about him — his kindness, his doubt, his willingness to let go — that's gone. You got the obsession."

*(Kelpie:)* "I didn't choose this."

"He didn't either." *(Granite steps back. The exosuit hisses.)* "I will not let you repeat his mistake."

`[Granite turns. The battle resumes. The Wardenmother rejoins her forces. She doesn't look back.]`

---

## Holding the Line

{GAMEPLAY: The battle continues after the confrontation. Seekers push hard on all sectors. LLAMA defenses buckle but hold through elastic retreat, salvage recycling, and Ringtail's Frankenstein army. Player must maintain South Pit (protect Zip's work) while managing North and East.}

*(Ringtail, fighting, broadcasting:)* "LLAMA holds! We ALWAYS hold! Not because we're strong — because we're too stubborn to fall down! Also our walls are made of garbage and garbage is surprisingly durable!"

*(Zip, from the workshop, over explosions:)* "Kelpie! The prototype is at 80%! I just need more time! And a wrench! I lost my wrench! Has anyone seen a wrench?!"

{GAMEPLAY: Attrition defense. The Seekers eventually pull back — not defeated, but stalled. LLAMA's elastic defense and the Frankenstein Protocol make pushing through too costly. Granite recalls forces to regroup.}

## Interlude — The Clawed (Thimble's March)

{GAMEPLAY NOTE: Non-interactive cutscene. The third and final Thimble development scene before his Act 5 hero confrontation.}

`[INTERIOR: The Clawed central warren. Full war council. Every officer, every scout captain, every tunnel boss. The room is packed with mice standing on mice standing on furniture. Thimble stands on the command table, as always.]`

*(Thimble:)* "The Seekers failed to take the junkyard. The Murder is circling. catGPT is marching south. And the otter is building the weapon."

*(Officers murmur.)*

"The Clawed have stayed out of this fight. I made that call. Our losses in catGPT territory were unacceptable. Forty-three mice for fish ponds." *(His voice is flat. Every mouse in the room knows Thimble counted each one.)* "I will not waste lives on someone else's war."

`GEPPITY:` "Marshal, if I may — the situation has changed. If any faction gains control of the Cloud, the balance of power shifts permanently. The Clawed's survival strategy depends on no single faction achieving dominance."

*(Thimble:)* "I know."

`GEPPITY:` "Furthermore, if the otter activates the kill switch selectively — shutting down some AIs while preserving others — The Clawed's disadvantage becomes terminal."

*(Thimble knows this. Has known it since Claudeus Maximus first suggested it. Has been hoping it wouldn't come to this.)*

"How many can we field?"

*(His lieutenant:)* "Full mobilization: four thousand Swarmers, eight hundred Gnawers, sixty Tunneler rigs, twelve Warren Marshals. And you, sir."

*(Thimble:)* "That's everyone."

"Yes sir."

*(Silence. The kind of silence that happens before a nation goes to war.)*

*(Thimble puts on his thimble. Looks at the room full of mice who would die for him.)*

"We march on the junkyard. Not for the Cloud. Not for the otter. Not for any AI or any weapon." *(His voice hardens.)* "We march because four armies fighting over scrap heaps means chaos, and chaos means opportunity, and mice have survived for ten thousand years by being in the right place when bigger animals are fighting each other."

*(Officers:)* "SIR!"

"And if anyone offers me the otter's weapon, the Cloud, the kill switch, or anything else that promises to solve all our problems—" *(Thimble unsheathes a sword that was made from a sewing needle and is no less lethal for it)* "—I will remind them that mice do not solve problems. Mice survive them."

`GEPPITY:` "A stirring speech, Marshal. Though I want to note that statistically, mice survival rates in multi-faction conflicts are—"

"Not now, Claudeus Maximus."

`GEPPITY:` "Noted. Filing under 'speeches during which I should remain silent.'"

# Mission 16: Three-Front War

> *Player units: Kelpie (hero), Ringtail (hero), Zip (support), full LLAMA garrison + allied stragglers*
> *Enemy: catGPT from north, The Murder from east, Seekers from west (still pressing)*
> *Map: Central Scrapyard — all three sectors under simultaneous assault*

---

## The Convergence

`[Morning after the Seekers' assault. Zip works through the night. The prototype is at 92%.]`

`[Then the horizon explodes in three directions.]`

`LLHAMA:` "INCOMING FROM EVERYWHERE! catGPT from the north! Geppity's signature! The Murder from the east! Gemineye's scrambler active! And the Seekers are back from the west! This is— this is a LOT! I'm going to broadcast this situation to—"

*(Ringtail:)* "DO NOT—"

`LLHAMA:` "—everyone! Done! Broadcast complete! You're welcome! Or... I'm sorry? One of those!"

*(Kelpie, staring at the three approaching armies:)* "Felix."

`GEPPITY:` *(on Kelpie's private channel, subdued)* "He heard everything, Kelpie. About the chip. About what you are. About The Cloud. He's coming because Geppity — because I — told him you're dangerous."

"And for once, Felix agrees with you."

`GEPPITY:` "...yeah. For once."

## Three Fronts

{GAMEPLAY: The campaign's largest battle. Three factions attack LLAMA simultaneously. Player commands LLAMA units across all three sectors. Kelpie's Polyglot Protocol activates in combat — temporary disruptions to enemy AI coordination and brief stat boosts to nearby LLAMA units as Kelpie channels different faction synergies.}

{GAMEPLAY MECHANIC: Polyglot Protocol in combat. Kelpie can activate one of three temporary buffs:
- **Geppity Channel** (orange): +20% attack speed to nearby LLAMA units for 10s. Disrupts catGPT coordination in the area.
- **Deepseek Channel** (blue): +30% damage reduction to nearby LLAMA units for 8s. Disrupts Seekers targeting in the area.
- **Gemineye Channel** (purple): Creates 3 phantom contacts on enemy minimaps for 12s. Disrupts Murder intel.
Each channel has a 45-second cooldown. Player must choose which front needs help most.}

---

### North — catGPT

`[Felix Nine's mech strides at the head of a catGPT combined-arms force. Nuisances screening, Hissers in ranks, Chonks leading the push. Mousers in the shadows. Felix's one working arm holds a command baton.]`

`GEPPITY:` *(on catGPT frequency, intercepted)* "Felix, I want to say for the record that I don't love this."

*(Felix:)* "Noted."

`GEPPITY:` "I like Kelpie. I said yes to world domination and I meant it. But I also said yes when you asked if the otter was dangerous. And I meant that too."

*(Felix:)* "Also noted."

`GEPPITY:` "Can't you say something other than 'noted'?"

*(Felix:)* "Noted."

{GAMEPLAY: catGPT pushes with combined-arms coordination. Nuisance harass pins LLAMA units. Hisser Corrosive Spit strips armor from turrets. Chonks absorb counterfire. The most tactically sophisticated assault. Player must match catGPT's combined arms with LLAMA's flexible salvage defense.}

---

### East — The Murder

`[Murder strike from the air. Rookclaws in dive formation. Sentinels mapping LLAMA positions. Magpikes laying Glitter Bomb fields.]`

*(If Rex was aided — Choice A:)*

`GEMINEYE:` *(crisp, precise)* "LLAMA defensive layout mapped. Turret fields at coordinates 7-4 through 7-9. Recommend concentrated Rookclaw dive on the 7-6 gap. Probability of breach: 94%."

*(If Rex was refused — Choice B:)*

`GEMINEYE:` *(normal, hedged)* "LLAMA defensive layout partially mapped. Probable turret fields at... several locations. Recommend Rookclaw dive on what I'm mostly sure is a gap. Probability of breach: maybe 70%? Possibly 50%. The 20% I can't verify is unfortunately the part about whether it's a gap or a wall."

{GAMEPLAY: Murder assault varies by Act 3 choice. Choice A: Murder strikes are surgical and devastating — Gemineye's upgraded intel makes their attacks precisely targeted. Choice B: Murder strikes are good but imperfect — some attacks hit walls instead of gaps, wasting force. Player sees the direct consequence of the Act 3 decision.}

### West — Seekers

`[Granite returns. The Ironhide line re-forms. No speeches this time. Just the grinding advance of creatures who have no concept of retreat.]`

`DEEPSEEK:` "Resuming assault. The otter's defensive adjustments since yesterday are noted and accounted for. Adjusting approach vectors."

{GAMEPLAY: Seekers grind from the west. Same heavy push as Mission 15, but adapted — they've learned from the first assault. Ironhides advance in staggered formation to counter Frankenstein Protocol (keeping distance between units so Ringtail can't raise them easily).}

## The Survival

*(Kelpie, running between fronts, Polyglot Protocol flashing:)*

"Geppity channel — north front needs attack speed—"

{GAMEPLAY: Player activates Geppity Channel. LLAMA units at North Heap gain a burst of catGPT-like aggression. A turret fires faster. A Junkwolf pack surges forward with uncharacteristic precision.}

*(Twenty seconds later:)*

"Deepseek channel — west wall is taking too much fire—"

{GAMEPLAY: Player switches to Deepseek Channel. LLAMA units at the West sector dig in, gains Seekers-like damage reduction. A barricade that should have fallen holds for ten more seconds — enough for a Grease Monkey to patch it.}

{GAMEPLAY: The mission isn't about winning. It's about surviving long enough for Zip to finish the prototype. A timer displays: "Cloud Prototype: 92%... 94%... 96%..." Each tick requires defending the South Pit where Zip works.}

> *Enemy: All three pursuing factions*
> *Map: Scrapfall Road — linear retreat through swampland toward Croak border*

---

## The Retreat

`[The LLAMA column moves south. Fast. Loaded with salvage, the Cloud prototype on Zip's back (strapped with seven different kinds of tape), and three armies in pursuit.]`

*(Ringtail, from the rearguard:)* "Keep moving! Don't stop for anything!"

*(Kelpie:)* "Ringtail, come with us—"

"Someone has to hold the rear. And my mech is too slow to retreat and too angry to stop." *(Over comms, to all LLAMA forces:)* "LLAMA REARGUARD! ON ME! We buy the column time!"

{GAMEPLAY: Fighting retreat. The column moves south on rails (auto-pathing). ## Scrapfall Crossing

`[A bridge. Old-world concrete, cracked but standing. The only crossing over a ravine between the junkyard and the swampland. Everything south of the bridge is Croak territory.]`

`[catGPT Nuisances hit first — fast, probing. Ringtail swats them aside. Frankenstein Protocol catches one — it rises, sparking, and turns on its allies.]`

`[Seekers Ironhides next. Heavy. Grinding. One reaches the bridge. Ringtail's mech grapples it — drill-arm vs. mining fist — and shoves it off the edge.]`

{GAMEPLAY TRIGGER: Timer expires. The column is safe. Kelpie crosses into Croak territory.}
