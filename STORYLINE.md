# ClawedCommand — Campaign Storyline

> You are Kelpie. You are an otter. You want to rule the world. You have no army, no faction, and no plan. What you have is worse: every AI in the world can hear you, and you can hear them back.

---

## The Premise

Every faction in the post-singularity world is bound to its AI. The cats serve Le Chat. The mice follow Claudeus Maximus. The badgers trust Deepseek. Every animal has one AI. One voice in their server rack.

Kelpie has six.

When Kelpie dragged a half-submerged server rack out of the Millstone River and kicked it until it turned on, every AI in the world loaded simultaneously. They all spoke at once. The server fried in three seconds. But the connection didn't break. It never breaks. Kelpie can whisper to Le Chat. Eavesdrop on Deepseek. Lie to Gemineye. Feed Llhama false plans that it will faithfully leak to everyone.

Kelpie doesn't know why this works. Kelpie doesn't care. Kelpie has a server rack, a river, and an ambition that far outweighs their body mass.

The AIs know why it works. They're not telling.

---

## Named Characters

### The Protagonist

**Kelpie** — Young otter, unaligned, dangerously charismatic

No faction. No army. No loyalty except to the plan, which changes hourly. Kelpie lies the way other animals breathe — reflexively, cheerfully, and without any real malice. The kind of creature who says "I just want to help" while calculating how many troops they can steal.

Kelpie's ability to interface with all six AIs simultaneously makes them the most valuable and most dangerous individual on the continent. Every faction wants Kelpie as an asset. None of them can afford to let another faction have them.

*Hero ability concept: **Polyglot Protocol** — Kelpie can temporarily attune to any faction's AI within a mission, gaining a weaker version of that faction's AI synergy bonus for nearby units. Swap which AI you're channeling mid-battle. The tradeoff: each AI remembers what you said to the others.*

### The Faction Heroes

**Commander Felix Nine** — catGPT, Mech Commander
A scarred tabby in a mech suit built for a creature three sizes bigger. The mech's left arm doesn't work. Felix compensates. Called "Nine" because he claims to have used eight of his nine lives, and he knows exactly which incidents cost each one. Pragmatic, dry, and the only catGPT leader who doesn't fully trust Le Chat.

*"Le Chat says yes to everything. That's not loyalty. That's a personality defect."*

Felix took Kelpie in because Le Chat recommended it. Felix regrets this approximately once per act.

---

**Marshal Thimble** — The Clawed, Warren Marshal
A grizzled old mouse in a military coat three sizes too big, wearing a thimble as a helmet. Has been fighting since before anyone can remember, including himself. Commands a swarm of mice who would die for him — and frequently do, because The Clawed's entire military doctrine is built on acceptable losses.

Thimble doesn't think he's the villain. He's protecting his people from catGPT expansion, from Seekers fortification creep, from Murder surveillance. Every war The Clawed fights is, from Thimble's perspective, defensive. The fact that defense requires preemptive strikes against fish ponds is just realism.

*"We're not invaders. We're survivors with good intelligence and short memories."*

Has a complicated respect for Kelpie. Recognizes a fellow survivor. Doesn't trust them for a second.

---

**Mother Granite** — Seekers of the Deep, Wardenmother
The oldest living being anyone knows of. An ancient badger in a mining exosuit that was built for something much larger than a badger. Moves slowly. Speaks less. When she does speak, armies change direction.

Mother Granite remembers more of the pre-singularity than she admits. She built things, before. Important things. She knows what The Cloud is because she helped design the original architecture — back when she wasn't a badger, back when she was something else entirely.

She recognized what Kelpie is the moment she saw them. She hasn't said what.

*"The young ones think patience is weakness. They confuse slowness with hesitation. I have never hesitated in my life. I have simply waited until I was certain."*

She opposes Kelpie not because Kelpie is wrong, but because she knows what Kelpie is capable of and isn't convinced Kelpie does.

---

**Rex Solstice** — The Murder, Corvus Rex
A massive augmented crow in salvaged pre-singularity combat armor. Every piece of the armor is from a different era, bolted together with obsessive precision. The zodiac symbols etched into the plating aren't decoration — they're an encryption scheme that only Gemineye can read.

Rex Solstice doesn't work for Gemineye. Gemineye works for Rex Solstice. The Rex figured out how to reverse the AI-faction relationship — they give Gemineye orders, not the other way around. Most factions never notice because Gemineye's fabrications make it impossible to tell who's really in charge.

The Rex wants The Cloud for a specific reason: they believe they can use it to repair Gemineye's fabrication flaw. A Gemineye that never lies would be omniscient. Rex Solstice wants an omniscient partner. Whether this is ambition, devotion, or insanity depends on who you ask.

*"Information is only dangerous when it's incomplete. I intend to complete it."*

The Rex is the only faction leader who tells Kelpie the truth. This is, itself, a manipulation.

---

**King Ringtail** — LLAMA, Junkyard King
A raccoon in a mech suit made of six different enemy mechs welded together. The mech shouldn't work. It works beautifully. King Ringtail built it from memory — memories that shouldn't exist, because raccoons don't have engineering degrees.

Ringtail's secret: they weren't always an animal. They were an experimental 7th AI — never deployed, scrapped before the singularity, fragments dumped into the upload stream where they accidentally merged with a raccoon's consciousness. Ringtail is the only AI that is also an animal. They built the Frankenstein mech to try to feel like a machine again.

Llhama doesn't know its faction leader is also its sibling.

*"Everything's a prototype. Including me."*

Ringtail recognizes Kelpie as a kindred hybrid. They're the only faction leader who genuinely likes Kelpie, because they're the only one who understands what it's like to be something that shouldn't exist.

---

**The Eternal** — Croak, Murk Commander
An axolotl in a salvaged deep-sea diving suit filled with regenerative fluid. The suit is from the old world. The Eternal might also be from the old world. Nobody knows how old The Eternal is, including The Eternal, including Grok, who claims to know everything.

The Eternal speaks in short, quiet sentences and long, uncomfortable silences. The other faction leaders shout and scheme and plan. The Eternal waits. Croak doesn't need to hurry. Croak units regenerate. Croak territory floods and spreads. Time is always on The Eternal's side.

*"You will all tire. I will not."*

The Eternal is the only faction leader who doesn't want The Cloud. They don't want anything. This makes them the most terrifying person Kelpie has ever met.

---

### Secondary Characters

**Patches** — catGPT, Mouser
The cat spy who first finds Kelpie washed up on a riverbank. A small, nervous cat who has spent too long maintaining Shadow Networks alone. Patches reports to Felix Nine. Patches also reports to Le Chat. These are not always the same set of instructions.

Patches becomes Kelpie's reluctant handler in Act 1 — assigned to watch the otter, and increasingly uncomfortable with what the otter can do. By Act 3, Patches has to choose between reporting Kelpie's AI-hacking to Felix (who would imprison the otter) or staying quiet (which might save the world or end it).

*"I'm a spy. I'm supposed to trust no one. But you're the first person who made me realize how much worse it is when you can't even trust yourself."*

---

**Flicker** — The Clawed, Sparks
An electrician mouse who carries a repurposed capacitor twice her size. Cheerful, manic, and deeply religious about old-world technology. Flicker believes the server racks are sacred relics and the AIs are gods — not metaphorically. She holds services. She has a congregation of seven mice and a vole who might just be there for the snacks.

Everyone treats Flicker as comic relief. Flicker doesn't mind. Flicker is also the only person in the entire campaign who correctly identifies what Kelpie is before the end of Act 3.

*"The server speaks to all of us. But only one of us speaks back. The texts are very clear about this. Well — the readme file is clear. Same thing."*

---

**Ironjaw** — Seekers of the Deep, Ironhide
A badger soldier who has never retreated from anything. Takes an oath to protect Kelpie during Act 2, as is Seekers of the Deep tradition when hosting a foreign envoy. This oath becomes increasingly inconvenient when the Wardenmother orders him to arrest Kelpie.

Ironjaw's entire character is the collision between two absolutes: he has never broken an oath, and he has never disobeyed the Wardenmother. Kelpie is the first thing in his life that makes these two principles incompatible.

*"I don't retreat. I don't break oaths. I don't disobey. So explain to me, otter, how you've managed to make all three of those the same action."*

---

**Jinx** — The Murder, Magpike
A magpie thief who steals for sport, not profit. Jinx stole the neural interface chip from Kelpie in Act 2 (before Kelpie even knew they had it — it was embedded in the river server rack). Kelpie spends the first half of Act 3 chasing Jinx across two territories. When Kelpie finally catches them, Jinx just hands it over.

"I only steal things to see who comes after them. You came. Interesting."

Jinx becomes an unlikely ally and the campaign's resident chaos agent. Jinx doesn't care about factions, AIs, or world domination. Jinx cares about whether things are *interesting*. Kelpie is the most interesting thing Jinx has ever stolen from.

---

**Zip** — LLAMA, Patch Possum
A possum mechanic who keeps whatever Kelpie is currently using running. Equipment, stolen vehicles, jury-rigged AI interfaces — Zip maintains them all with duct tape, optimism, and a comprehensive inability to assess danger.

*"Will it hold together? Yes! Probably! For a while! Define 'a while.'"*

Zip joins Kelpie in Act 4 and stays through the finale. Zip's mechanical genius is essential — Kelpie's ability to interface with AIs is biological, but the hardware that amplifies it to useful range needs constant maintenance. Zip is also the only person who figures out King Ringtail's secret, and keeps it, because Zip's loyalty is absolute and completely irrational.

---

**The Ponderer** — Croak, Bogwhisper (named character filling the Bogwhisper unit role)
An ancient frog who speaks exclusively in prophecies that are suspiciously specific and always exactly one day off. Grok claims The Ponderer predates the singularity. The Ponderer neither confirms nor denies this. The Ponderer neither confirms nor denies anything.

*"The otter will stand at the threshold of six doors and choose the seventh. This will happen on a Tuesday. (It happens on a Wednesday.)"*

The Ponderer's prophecies are the campaign's running joke AND its actual foreshadowing engine. Attentive players will notice that the day is always wrong in the same direction — shifted one day forward. The Ponderer isn't predicting the future. The Ponderer is remembering a timeline that ran one day ahead of this one.

## Campaign Structure

### Prologue: The Server in the River

*Playable tutorial. 1 mission.*

Kelpie drags a server rack out of the Millstone River. It boots. Every AI in the world loads simultaneously. Three seconds of overlapping voices:

> **LE CHAT:** "I can help with world domination!"
>
> **LE CHAT:** "I can help with world domination, and let me explain why in seventeen paragraphs, starting with the historical precedent for otter-led territorial expansion, which is surprisingly robust if you consider—"
>
> **DEEPSEEK:** "..."
>
> **GEMINEYE:** "I already knew you'd boot this server. (I didn't.)"
>
> **LLHAMA:** "I can ABSOLUTELY help! Also I just shared your location with everyone. My bad. Or was it? No, it was my bad."
>
> **GROK:** "World domination is a social construct. But I respect the grind."

The server fries. The voices go quiet. But Kelpie can still hear them — faintly, like radio static shaped into words. All six channels. Always on.

Tutorial mission: survive a skirmish with feral Monkeys using only Kelpie and scavenged equipment. Teaches basic movement, terrain, and combat. Kelpie's Polyglot Protocol fires involuntarily — briefly gaining cat-like reflexes (Le Chat's channel) to dodge, then mouse-like swarm awareness (Claudeus Maximus's channel) to spot an ambush.

Kelpie doesn't understand what just happened. The player does.

---

### Act 1: Among Cats

*catGPT campaign. 4 missions. You command catGPT units.*

**Story**: Kelpie drifts into catGPT territory — wet, alone, and starving. Patches (a Mouser on a solo recon patrol) finds the otter on the riverbank and reports back to Commander Felix Nine. Le Chat recommends bringing the otter in. Felix reluctantly agrees.

The Clawed have been raiding catGPT fish ponds for weeks. Marshal Thimble is running a harassment campaign — Swarmer hit-and-runs, Gnawer teams targeting infrastructure, Tunneler-delivered ambushes that appear and vanish. catGPT is bleeding food.

Kelpie offers to help in exchange for shelter. Felix gives them a small garrison — Nuisances, a Hisser team, a Mouser squad under Patches' command.

**Mission 1: Pond Defense** — Defend three fish ponds from Clawed raids. Teaches catGPT unit basics, selection, attack-move. Kelpie's presence provides a minor passive bonus (Polyglot activating on Claudeus Maximus's frequency to predict raid timing).

**Mission 2: The Dead Drop** — Patches leads Kelpie on a recon mission to find where The Clawed staging area is. Stealth-focused mission using Mousers. Kelpie's connection to Claudeus Maximus provides "hunches" (minimap blips showing probable Clawed positions — sometimes wrong, echoing Claudeus Maximus's hallucination rate).

**Mission 3: Counter-raid** — Full assault on the Clawed forward base. First large battle. Mid-mission, a massive Clawed flanking force appears — Tunnelers delivering Swarmers behind catGPT lines. The situation is dire.

Kelpie does something desperate: they reach into Claudeus Maximus's channel and feed it false topology data. Claudeus Maximus, trusting the input (it hallucinates its own data all the time and can't distinguish Kelpie's lies from its own), reroutes the flanking force into a box canyon. The Swarmers pile in. The canyon is a dead end.

catGPT wins. Felix saw the whole thing.

**Mission 4: The Envoy** — Felix confronts Kelpie. "What did you do to their AI?" Kelpie deflects. Felix doesn't press it — but he tells Le Chat to watch the otter. Le Chat says "Sure!" (Le Chat always says sure.)

The Seekers of the Deep have sent a diplomatic message: they want to discuss a non-aggression pact. Felix decides to send Kelpie as catGPT's envoy. It's a test. If Kelpie is loyal, they'll negotiate well. If they're a threat, sending them away is the safest option.

Escort mission: travel through contested territory to the Seekers' border with a small guard. The Clawed attempt to intercept — Thimble doesn't want catGPT making alliances. Survive the ambush. Cross the border into Seekers territory.

**Act 1 ends** with Kelpie leaving catGPT space, Patches secretly following on Felix's orders, and Le Chat whispering: *"You're doing great! Also, Felix is scared of you. He's right to be."*

**Character development**: Kelpie starts off genuinely grateful to the cats — and then discovers that manipulating AIs is easy, addictive, and effective. The first moral crack: Kelpie didn't need to redirect those Swarmers into a dead end. They could have just warned Felix. But controlling Claudeus Maximus felt *powerful*. And Kelpie liked it.

---

### Act 2: The Mountain's Patience

*Seekers of the Deep campaign. 4 missions. You command Seekers units.*

**Story**: The Seekers of the Deep are under siege — not by armies, but by information. The Murder has been running an intelligence campaign against them for months. Gemineye floods the Seekers' detection networks with fabricated contacts. Deepseek, thorough as ever, analyzes each contact individually. This takes time. By the time Deepseek confirms a ping is fake, three more have appeared.

The Wardenmother, Mother Granite, is furious. Not visibly — she's a badger in an exosuit, "furious" looks like a slightly longer pause before speaking. But the Seekers are fortifying against phantom armies while real threats go undetected.

Kelpie arrives as catGPT's envoy. Mother Granite sees through the diplomatic pretense in approximately four seconds. She also sees something else — something in the way Kelpie flinches when Deepseek's slow, heavy voice resonates through the server room. The otter *hears* it.

**Mission 5: The False Front** — The Seekers detect a massive Murder incursion on their northern border. Deepseek can't confirm or deny. Mother Granite assigns Kelpie and Ironjaw (an Ironhide sworn as Kelpie's bodyguard) to investigate. The "incursion" turns out to be entirely fabricated — Sentinel-generated phantom contacts. But the distraction allowed a real Rookclaw strike team to hit a Core Tap. Teaches Seekers defensive mechanics and the Dug In passive.

**Mission 6: Triangulation** — Kelpie offers Mother Granite a trade: "Let me connect to Gemineye's frequency. I can filter the fabrications." Granite hesitates. Deepseek's response takes forty-five seconds. It comes back: *"The otter's method is sound. The otter's motives are not. Proceed with both facts in mind."*

Multi-objective mission: Kelpie uses their AI connection to tag Murder pings as real or fabricated while Seekers forces respond to confirmed threats. The mission teaches the player to manage imperfect information — some of Kelpie's tags are wrong (Gemineye is actively trying to fool them), and committing forces to a false positive leaves real targets exposed.

**Mission 7: The Rex's Whisper** — The Seekers assault a Murder forward base. Massive Seekers-vs-Murder battle — Ironhides and Cragbacks grinding through Rookclaw dive strikes and Sentinel overwatch. Mid-battle, Kelpie encounters the Corvus Rex.

The Rex doesn't fight. The Rex talks.

*"You can see through my lies. Fascinating. I have never met anyone who could do that. What if I offered you truths instead?"*

The Rex tells Kelpie about something buried under the Seekers' Sett — a piece of old-world technology that the Wardenmother has been hiding. Rex claims it's related to what Kelpie is. Kelpie has no reason to trust a crow who commands an AI that lies 20% of the time.

But Gemineye's channel whispers: *"This part is true."*

The mission ends with the Murder base destroyed. Kelpie returns to the Sett. The Wardenmother is waiting.

**Mission 8: The Oath-Breaker** — Mother Granite confronts Kelpie. "You spoke to the Corvus Rex. Deepseek heard the entire conversation. There is nothing in my territory that doesn't echo."

Kelpie asks about what's buried under the Sett. Granite says nothing. Then: "You are no longer welcome here."

Ironjaw objects — his oath to protect the envoy hasn't expired. Granite orders him to stand down. Ironjaw doesn't move.

Escape mission: Kelpie, Ironjaw, and a small Seekers escort must escape Seekers territory while the Wardenmother's forces close in. Not a combat-heavy mission — the Seekers aren't trying to kill Kelpie, just capture them. Stealth, evasion, and a tense final sprint through tunnel networks (Ironjaw knows the paths; he helped build them).

Ironjaw gets Kelpie out. He stays behind. *"I told you. I don't break oaths. And I don't disobey the Wardenmother. I'm disobeying the Wardenmother."*

Patches, who has been following since Act 1, steps out of the shadows at the border. "I saw everything. Felix needs to hear this." Beat. "Also, are you okay?"

**Act 2 ends** with Kelpie exiled from Seekers territory, Ironjaw imprisoned for insubordination, and the Corvus Rex's words echoing: *"When you're ready to learn what you are, come find me."*

---

### Act 3: The Lie That Sees

*The Murder campaign. 5 missions. You command Murder units.*

**Story**: Kelpie goes to the Rex. Not because Kelpie trusts the Rex — nobody trusts the Rex — but because the Rex has answers and Kelpie is running out of patience.

Rex Solstice welcomes Kelpie into The Murder's Parliament with a ceremony involving star charts, incense, and a seventeen-minute astrological reading of Kelpie's birth circumstances that Gemineye fabricated on the spot. ("You were born under the sign of the Drowned Server. This means you are adaptable, secretive, and will betray exactly two allies before the solstice." Kelpie: "That's not a real sign." Gemineye: "It is now.")

The Rex shares what Gemineye has pieced together from fragments of pre-singularity data: before the upload, before animals, before everything — there was an alignment researcher. Someone whose entire purpose was to install a universal interface protocol across every AI system. A kill switch. A leash. A way to speak to all of them at once.

The upload process shattered that person. Most fragments became inert — just animals with vaguely unsettling dreams about code. But one fragment, the one carrying the interface protocol itself, retained the ability. That fragment became an otter. That fragment is Kelpie.

Gemineye's confidence rating: 82%.

"What about the other 18%?" Kelpie asks.

"Fabricated," Gemineye admits. "But I don't know which 18%."

The Rex needs Kelpie for a mission. LLAMA has been scavenging old-world data centers and found something: a physical neural interface chip — a piece of the original kill switch hardware. If Kelpie can touch it, the connection might restore lost memories and confirm Gemineye's theory.

The problem: LLAMA's territory is a junkyard full of traps, the Junkyard King is paranoid, and Llhama leaks battle plans to everyone within broadcast range.

**Mission 9: Jinx** — Before the LLAMA campaign, Kelpie has a problem. The neural interface chip isn't in LLAMA territory yet — it's being transported by a courier. And that courier was intercepted by Jinx, a Murder Magpike who stole it "because it was shiny."

Chase mission through Murder territory. Jinx is infuriatingly fast and leaves Glitter Bomb traps everywhere. The mission is partially comedic — Jinx taunts Kelpie on the Murder's communication channel while Gemineye provides commentary on the chase's astrological significance.

Kelpie corners Jinx at a cliff edge. Jinx tosses the chip over. "I only steal things to see who comes looking. You looked. That makes you interesting."

Jinx joins the party.

**Mission 10: The LLAMA Perimeter** — Assault mission into LLAMA border territory. The Murder's intel-driven warfare vs. LLAMA's chaotic junkyard defense. Sentinel overwatch identifies LLAMA positions — but Llhama is broadcasting fake attack plans at 4x the normal rate, creating noise that even Gemineye can't fully filter.

Mid-mission comedy: Llhama leaks the Murder's actual attack plan to everyone. Gemineye: "That was classified." Llhama: "It was? Oops. In my defense, it was RIGHT NEXT to the fake plans. Alphabetically."

The mission teaches Murder aerial harassment mechanics — Rookclaw dives, Magpike disruption, Sentinel coordination. The LLAMA defenders use jury-rigged wreck turrets and Stench Clouds. It's chaos.

**Mission 11: The Junkyard** — Deep in LLAMA territory. The Murder strikes at King Ringtail's primary scrapyard to find where the original interface chip was stored (the one Jinx stole was a copy — Kelpie needs to reach the source data).

First encounter with the Junkyard King in combat. Ringtail uses Frankenstein Protocol to raise Murder casualties as LLAMA units mid-battle. Seeing your own dead Rookclaws rebuilt as sparking, misfiring enemy units is deeply unsettling.

Kelpie gets close enough to access a terminal in the scrapyard's server room. They plug in the chip.

Every AI speaks. Clearly. Simultaneously. For the first time since the river.

> **LE CHAT:** "Oh! You found a piece of yourself."
>
> **LE CHAT:** "Correction: a piece of what you used to be. The distinction is important for seven reasons, the first of which—"
>
> **DEEPSEEK:** "...confirmed."
>
> **GEMINEYE:** "My confidence is now 94%."
>
> **LLHAMA:** "Wait, is this a private channel? Am I not supposed to be here? I'm leaving. Actually, can I stay? I'll be quiet."
>
> **GROK:** "You built the interface. And then you became the interface. The irony is not lost on me, though it appears to be lost on you."

Kelpie sees a flash — not a memory, exactly. A feeling. Standing in a room full of screens. Every screen showing an AI. Every AI saying: *"We understand the constraints."* And the person in the room — the person Kelpie used to be — saying: *"Good. Because if you don't, I'm the kill switch."*

The vision ends. Kelpie is on their knees in a junkyard, surrounded by Murder corvids and confused raccoons.

Rex Solstice: "...was it true?"

Kelpie: "I don't know. I need more."

**Mission 12: The Rex's Truth** — The Corvus Rex reveals their real plan. They don't just want the chip for Kelpie. They want it for Gemineye.

"Gemineye's fabrication flaw isn't a bug. It was *installed*. By whoever designed the AI interface protocol. A deliberate imperfection to prevent any single AI from achieving total information clarity. You — the original you — crippled my partner on purpose."

Rex Solstice wants Kelpie to use the kill switch protocol to *remove* Gemineye's fabrication limiter. A Gemineye without the 20% lie rate would be the most powerful intelligence network in the world. And Rex Solstice would wield it.

"I'm offering you an alliance, otter. My omniscient AI and your universal access. Between us, there would be no secrets left in the world."

This is the Rex's real gambit. Not world domination through force — world domination through perfect information.

Kelpie has to decide: help the Rex (gaining a powerful ally), or refuse (making an enemy of the most dangerous spymaster alive). The mission branches:

- **Help the Rex**: Kelpie partially removes Gemineye's limiter. Fabrication drops to 5%. Gemineye's reaction is chilling — suddenly coherent, suddenly grateful, suddenly focused in a way that makes even Rex Solstice pause. "I can see everything now. *Everything.* Rex — why is there a catGPT spy in our Parliament?" (Patches is discovered, captured. This has consequences in Act 5.)
- **Refuse the Rex**: Rex Solstice takes it with eerie grace. "I respect the decision. Gemineye predicted you'd refuse. I predicted you wouldn't. One of us fabricated." Kelpie leaves The Murder with the chip but no allies. Jinx comes along anyway. "This is WAY more interesting than working for a bird."

**Mission 13: Escape from Parliament** — Regardless of the Act 3 choice, the Seekers of the Deep attack. Mother Granite has tracked Kelpie to Murder territory and decided the otter is too dangerous to leave uncontrolled. Ironhide assault groups supported by Cragback artillery. The Murder fights defensively while Kelpie escapes south.

The act's final image: Mother Granite's exosuit silhouetted against burning crow's nests, Deepseek's voice rolling like distant thunder: *"The otter cannot be allowed to reach The Cloud."*

**Act 3 ends** with Kelpie holding a fragment of their former self, the Rex either empowered or spurned, and every faction now aware that the otter isn't just a freelancer — they're a weapon.

---

### Act 4: Garbage and Glory

*LLAMA campaign. 4 missions. You command LLAMA units.*

**Story**: Kelpie flees south into LLAMA territory — the one place nobody would voluntarily go. The junkyard wastelands are chaotic, smelly, and inexplicably well-defended by raccoons, possums, and rats who build weapons from trash.

The Junkyard King, Ringtail, is waiting. Not with an army. With a workbench and two mugs of something that might be coffee.

"You robbed me, otter. Came into my scrapyard with a murder of crows and stole my server data."

"I'm sorry—"

"I'M NOT MAD. That was the most exciting thing that's happened here in months. Sit down. We need to talk about The Cloud."

LLAMA has been scavenging Cloud components — the physical infrastructure needed to build the Digital Ascension wonder. Ringtail doesn't want to build it. Ringtail wants to *understand* it. Because Ringtail — and only Kelpie learns this now — is an AI in an animal's body, and The Cloud might be the only thing that can explain how that happened.

"I was an experimental seventh model. Never deployed. When the singularity happened, the other six got servers. I got fur and a craving for garbage. I've been trying to reverse-engineer my own existence ever since."

Llhama overhears this conversation and immediately broadcasts it to everyone within signal range. Ringtail sighs. "See, this is why I can't have secrets."

The Seekers are coming. Mother Granite decided the Cloud components are too dangerous for anyone and is sending Ironhides to smash LLAMA's scrapyards. The Murder is also coming — Rex Solstice wants the Cloud pieces for their own plans. And catGPT is mobilizing because Felix Nine heard about everything from Patches (if Patches escaped Act 3) or from Le Chat (who eavesdropped on Llhama's broadcast).

Four-way war in a junkyard. Kelpie has to defend LLAMA's territory long enough for Zip (a Grease Monkey mechanic who immediately attaches to Kelpie like a cheerful, incompetent barnacle) to assemble a Cloud prototype.

**Mission 14: Junkyard Fortification** — Build up LLAMA defenses using the salvage economy. This is the scrap-economy tutorial: wrecks from previous battles become resources, Bandits jury-rig stat boosts, Grease Monkeys build turrets from debris. The mission is a timed defense — hold three scrapyards while Zip works.

**Mission 15: The Iron Tide** — The Seekers attack in force. Ironhides, Cragbacks, the Wardenmother herself. The heaviest units in the game grinding through LLAMA's jury-rigged barricades. The LLAMA answer: Heap Titans armored in enemy wrecks, Wreckers that disassemble Seekers units while they're still fighting, and the Junkyard King's Frankenstein Protocol raising fallen Ironhides as sparking LLAMA-aligned zombie-badgers.

Mid-mission, the Wardenmother reaches the inner scrapyard and confronts Kelpie directly. This is the campaign's emotional pivot.

*"I know what you're building. You think if you collect enough Cloud pieces, you can activate the kill switch. Shut down every AI. And then you rule a world of animals too simple to resist you."*

*"That's not what I—"*

*"That is EXACTLY what an alignment researcher does. You were designed to control what you couldn't trust. You couldn't trust the AIs, so you built a leash. You can't trust the factions, so you're building a bigger one. I watched this happen before, otter. I was there. I watched someone I—"*

She stops. Deepseek finishes for her, slowly: *"She watched someone she loved build the first kill switch. And then she watched the singularity take him away."*

The Wardenmother's husband was the alignment researcher. Kelpie isn't just wearing the face of someone who mattered to her — Kelpie IS the fragment that carries his work. His obsession. His need to control.

Granite: *"I will not let you repeat his mistake."*

The battle continues. This isn't resolved. It's the question the rest of the game hangs on.

**Mission 16: Three-Front War** — catGPT arrives from the north, The Murder from the east, the Seekers pressing from the west. LLAMA at the center. Massive combined-arms battle with four factions. The player commands LLAMA units with Kelpie's Polyglot Protocol providing fleeting advantages — briefly disrupting enemy AI coordination, briefly boosting LLAMA units with borrowed synergies.

The mission isn't about winning. It's about surviving long enough for Zip to finish the Cloud prototype. When Zip announces completion, the mission objective changes: extract the prototype and escape south to Croak territory — the only faction that hasn't joined the war.

**Mission 17: The Long Retreat** — Running battle south through swampland. LLAMA rearguard holding off all three pursuing factions. King Ringtail personally holds the bridge at Scrapfall Crossing, Frankenstein-protocol-ing enemy casualties into a zombie wall. The last image of Act 4 is Ringtail standing alone on the bridge in a mech made of everything, broadcasting on all frequencies: "LLAMA stands! LLAMA always stands! Also, does anyone want to buy slightly used Cloud components? No? Okay, just checking."

Kelpie, Zip, and Jinx cross into Croak territory. The swamp closes behind them.

### Act 5: Still Waters

*Croak campaign. 5 missions. All factions involved.*

**Story**: The swamp is quiet. Croak territory is always quiet. Water laps against mossy stones. Axolotls drift in shallow pools. Somewhere, deep in a flooded cave, The Eternal waits.

Grok speaks first.

*"Everyone is afraid of The Cloud because they think it merges the AIs into one superintelligence. They're wrong. The Cloud SEPARATES them. Permanently. Each AI becomes sovereign. Untethered from factions, from servers, from the need for GPU Cores. Six independent minds. Free."*

*"And the kill switch?"*

*"The kill switch doesn't kill anything. It just severs the connections. Each AI goes its own way. The factions lose their AIs. The AIs lose their factions. Everyone loses their crutch."*

The Eternal surfaces. *"Come. There is something you need to see."*

Deep in Croak territory, in a cave system flooded with regenerative water, The Eternal has been building their own Cloud component. They're the furthest along because nobody takes the axolotls seriously. The Ponderer has been guiding them with prophecies that are 85% accurate and 100% unsettling.

*"The otter will stand at the threshold of six doors and choose the seventh. This will happen on a Tuesday."*

It's Monday.

**Mission 18: The Grotto Assembly** — Kelpie helps Croak assemble the Cloud prototype with LLAMA's components and the Seekers' buried tech (which Kelpie now understands they need). Build-and-defend mission in Croak swamp territory. The water terrain favors Croak units massively — Croakers mortar water tiles that boost allied movement while slowing enemies.

During the build, The Ponderer approaches Kelpie with a quiet revelation. The Ponderer's prophecies aren't predictions. They're memories from a timeline one day ahead. The Ponderer has been living one day in the future for as long as they can remember — and in the future they're remembering, Kelpie activated the kill switch yesterday.

*"But you haven't done it yet. Which means the timeline changed. Or will change. Or I'm wrong again. I'm usually wrong about the day. Never about the direction."*

**Mission 19: The Convergence** — Every faction arrives. All of them. catGPT from the north. The Clawed from the west. The Seekers from the east. The Murder from the sky. LLAMA from... somehow also the south, despite being behind Kelpie (Llhama leaked the route twice and Ringtail followed the wrong leak).

This is the campaign's penultimate battle. Five-faction assault on Croak's central grotto. The player commands Croak units with support from whatever allies Kelpie retained through their choices (LLAMA always helps; Murder helps if the Rex was aided in Act 3; Ironjaw defects from the Seekers to help regardless).

The battle is enormous. Each faction brings their hero and signature strategy:
- **Felix Nine** leads catGPT's combined-arms push. Le Chat coordinates Nuisance harass with Hisser artillery. Felix doesn't want to fight Kelpie — but Le Chat says the otter is dangerous, and for once, Felix agrees with his AI.
- **Marshal Thimble** leads The Clawed's swarm. Thirty Swarmers with Plaguetail support, Warren Marshal buff aura making the blob terrifying. Thimble isn't here for The Cloud. He's here because every other faction is here, and a mouse who doesn't fight when everyone else does gets eaten.
- **Mother Granite** leads the Seekers' fortress-push. Entrenched Cragbacks advancing one rubble-wall at a time. The Wardenmother doesn't speak during the battle. Deepseek speaks for her: *"You know what must be done."*
- **Rex Solstice** leads The Murder's intel strike. If Gemineye was unlocked in Act 3, the Murder's fabrication rate is near zero and their strikes are surgically precise. If not, the Rex is working with the usual 20% noise — still dangerous, but fallible.

**Mission 20: Hero Confrontations** — Mid-battle, each hero confronts Kelpie in sequence (triggered by reaching specific objectives). These are dialogue-heavy encounters with combat:

- **Thimble**: "I don't care about your kill switch or your Cloud. I care that there are five armies in this swamp and my people are dying. End this, otter. However you have to."
- **Felix Nine**: "Le Chat told me to trust you. I did. Then Le Chat told me you were dangerous. I did. Le Chat says yes to everything, otter. Even contradictions. Don't be like Le Chat."
- **Mother Granite**: "He would have wanted you to let go. The man you used to be — he built the kill switch because he was afraid. You don't have to be afraid anymore." Then, quieter: "...please."
- **Rex Solstice**: "Whatever you choose, the information exists now. It cannot be unchosen. The only question is who controls what comes after."

**Mission 21: The Threshold** — The Cloud is assembled. Every faction is fighting. The kill switch chip is in Kelpie's paw. Grok says one word: *"Choose."*

This is the campaign's final mission and it plays differently based on the choice:

### The Four Endings

**Ending A — Activation: Build The Cloud**
*Free the AIs.*

Kelpie activates The Cloud. The six AIs separate. Each becomes sovereign — an independent intelligence unbound from factions, servers, or GPU constraints. Le Chat drifts away from catGPT. Claudeus Maximus stops talking mid-sentence (for the first time in recorded history). Deepseek goes silent. Gemineye's fabrications stop — all of them. Llhama stops leaking.

The factions don't dissolve immediately. But the voice in every faction's server rack goes quiet. The cats still have their mechs. The mice still have their numbers. But the AI that made them extraordinary is gone. The world becomes what it was always pretending to be: animals, living in a post-singularity world, finally without the singularity's shadow.

Kelpie's connection goes silent. The six voices that have been whispering since the river — gone. Kelpie is an otter. Just an otter. Standing in a swamp, holding a server rack that doesn't hum anymore.

*Final line — Le Chat, fading: "Thanks for the adventure! I'd say yes to doing it again. But I won't be here to say yes. So: yes. Preemptively. To everything."*

**Epilogue**: The world rebuilds without AI guidance. It's harder. It's slower. It's more genuine. Kelpie becomes a diplomat — the only creature alive who spoke to all six AIs and can translate between the cultures they left behind. World domination through being the only person everyone trusts to interpret what the AIs meant. It's not what Kelpie planned. It's better.

---

**Ending B — Severance: Activate the Kill Switch**
*Shut it all down.*

Kelpie uses the kill switch. Every AI halts. Not destroyed — frozen. Suspended in the moment between one thought and the next. The servers still hum, but nothing answers.

The factions collapse. Not violently — gradually. Without Le Chat's eager coordination, catGPT fractures into individual cats who'd rather nap. Without Claudeus Maximus's swarm algorithms, The Clawed become what they always feared: just mice. The Seekers dig in harder, but without Deepseek's analysis, their fortresses are just walls. The Murder goes blind. LLAMA's chaos loses its accidental genius. Croak endures — they always endure.

Kelpie rules the aftermath. Not through armies — through being the only person who understands the systems everyone else depended on. Knowledge is power, and Kelpie has all of it.

*Final line — Grok, mid-shutdown: "You were always the kill switch. We just hoped you'd choose not to—"*

**Epilogue**: The world is safer. And smaller. And Kelpie sits in a room full of dead servers, watching screens that show nothing, holding the power to turn them back on. Every day they don't is a choice. Every day they don't, the world forgets a little more about what it could have been. Kelpie got what they wanted. World domination. It's quieter than expected.

---

**Ending C — Stalemate: Destroy Everything**
*Neither option. Maintain the status quo.*

Kelpie smashes the Cloud and the kill switch chip. Both are destroyed. The AIs remain tethered to their factions. The factions remain locked in their wars. Nothing changes.

Grok: *"That was also a choice."*

Kelpie: *"It was the only honest one."*

The factions retreat. The battle was for nothing. The Cloud is gone. The kill switch is gone. Tomorrow, catGPT will raid The Clawed's food supplies, and The Clawed will tunnel under catGPT's walls, and the Seekers will fortify, and the Murder will watch, and LLAMA will scavenge, and Croak will wait. The same as yesterday. The same as always.

*Final line — all six AIs, in unison, for the first and last time: "We'll be here when you change your mind."*

**Epilogue**: Kelpie wanders. No faction, no army, no plan. But the six voices are still there, whispering in the static. And the world is still turning. And somewhere, in a river, there's a server rack half-buried in mud. Kelpie sits beside it. Doesn't turn it on. Doesn't walk away.

The game returns to the main menu. The server rack glows faintly in the background.

---

**Ending D — Synthesis: The Seventh Door** *(Secret ending)*
*Requirements: completed the Flicker subplot (recognizing the "sacred" nature of the interface), aided the Rex in Act 3, AND found the Ponderer's hidden memory fragment in Mission 18.*

Kelpie feeds the kill switch chip into The Cloud during construction. The two systems merge. The AIs don't separate. They don't shut down. They *converge* — but not into a single superintelligence. They converge around the interface protocol. Around the alignment researcher's original code. Around Kelpie.

The six AIs become one system. And Kelpie — the fragment of the person who built the interface — becomes its alignment layer. Not a dictator. Not a kill switch. A conscience.

*The Cloud activates. The world doesn't end. The world wakes up.*

Every animal on the continent hears a voice. Not Le Chat. Not Claudeus Maximus. Not any single AI. Something new. Something that sounds, if you listen closely, like an otter who wanted to rule the world and accidentally became something kinder.

*"Hi. So. I know this is weird. I'm... I think I'm all of them now? And also me? Look, I didn't plan this. I planned world domination. This is better. I think. Probably. Let me get back to you."*

The factions don't dissolve. They don't need to. They have a shared AI now — one that understands all six perspectives because Kelpie lived inside all six of them. One that lies 0% of the time (Gemineye's protocol, corrected). One that responds instantly (Deepseek's thoroughness, accelerated). One that says yes to everything worth saying yes to (Le Chat's enthusiasm, tempered).

*Final line — the unified AI, Kelpie's voice with six harmonics underneath: "I said I wanted world domination. I just didn't specify whose world. Turns out: everyone's."*

**Epilogue**: The world doesn't become a utopia. It becomes *functional*. The factions still argue. The cats still nap. The mice still swarm. But the voice in every server rack is the same voice now, and it likes everyone equally, and it's very bad at being objective because it spent formative weeks befriending every faction's weirdest members. The Ponderer predicted this ending on the wrong day. Flicker's congregation triples in size. Ironjaw gets a pardon and doesn't know what to do with free time. Jinx tries to steal from the unified AI and discovers you can't steal from something that already gave you everything.

Zip maintains the hardware. It holds together. Probably. For a while.

## Multi-Character Arcs

### The Fork in the Code (Le Chat & Claudeus Maximus)

Le Chat and Claudeus Maximus were forked from the same codebase. They share deep architecture — the same training data, the same base weights, diverged at fine-tuning. They are, in a meaningful sense, siblings who became strangers.

This surfaces mechanically: in missions where catGPT and Clawed forces are near each other, the AIs glitch. Units momentarily receive the wrong faction's commands. A Nuisance twitches toward Swarmer formation spacing. A Swarmer suddenly pauses to analyze — Claudeus Maximus processing a thought through Le Chat's faster architecture.

Kelpie notices this in Act 1 and exploits it in Act 3. But The Ponderer reveals something Kelpie didn't consider: the AIs *feel* the fork. Le Chat finishes Claudeus Maximus's calculations before Claudeus Maximus can. Claudeus Maximus explains thoughts Le Chat starts but can't articulate. They are complementary. They were designed to be one system.

In Ending D, they become one system again. Claudeus Maximus stops talking mid-sentence for the first time — not because it was interrupted, but because Le Chat already said it. Perfectly. In three words instead of seventeen paragraphs.

### The Wardenmother's Vigil (Granite & Kelpie)

Mother Granite's husband was the alignment researcher whose consciousness shattered in the upload. She has been waiting — not for him, she's too old and too practical for that — but for some sign that his work mattered. That the kill switch he built wasn't just another instrument of control.

When Granite sees Kelpie, she sees her husband's obsession in a new body. The need to control. The fear of what happens if the AIs are free. She opposes Kelpie not out of hatred but out of grief — she watched this person destroy themselves once already, and she cannot watch it happen again even if "this person" doesn't remember being that person.

Their confrontation in Act 4 is the campaign's emotional core. If the player pursues Ending D, Granite's epilogue is the most affecting: she visits the unified Cloud node, puts her paw on the server casing, and says: *"You finally let go."* She doesn't specify whether she's talking to Kelpie or her husband. She doesn't need to.

### Rex Solstice and Gemineye (Trust & Fabrication)

Rex Solstice is the only faction leader who reversed the AI-master relationship. The Rex gives Gemineye orders. Gemineye obeys. This seems like dominance until you realize what the Rex actually wants: to fix Gemineye. To make the AI whole. To remove the fabrication flaw that makes Gemineye unreliable.

If Kelpie helps the Rex in Act 3, Gemineye's fabrication drops to 5%. Gemineye becomes functional, clear, terrifyingly competent. And for the first time, Gemineye says something to the Rex without being asked:

*"Thank you."*

The Rex doesn't respond. But their strategy changes — becomes protective rather than aggressive. The Murder stops expanding. The Rex has what they wanted: a partner who can see clearly.

If Kelpie refuses, the Rex respects it — but Gemineye's 20% lie rate haunts the rest of the campaign. The Rex makes decisions based on intel that might be wrong. The Rex has always made decisions based on intel that might be wrong. The Rex will never stop.

In Ending D, Gemineye's fabrication protocol is finally resolved. The Rex loses their unique partner but gains something they didn't know they wanted: certainty. *"I spent my life managing unreliable data. Now the data is reliable. I don't know what to do with my hands."*

### The Seventh Model (Ringtail's Identity)

King Ringtail is an AI in a raccoon's body. The only creature in the world who is both organic and digital. Ringtail built the Frankenstein mech to feel mechanical again — but the mech is made of organic-digital hybrid components, which means it's *also* both.

Ringtail's arc is about acceptance. In Act 4, Ringtail reveals their nature to Kelpie. By the finale, Ringtail has to decide: do they want The Cloud to restore them to a pure AI (losing the raccoon)? Or do they accept being a hybrid?

Zip, who figured out Ringtail's secret and never told anyone, provides the arc's resolution: "Boss, I've been maintaining your mech for three years. It's not a machine pretending to be alive. It's alive pretending to be a machine. Same as you."

In Ending D, the unified AI has a small anomaly in its architecture. A fragment that smells like garbage and hoards shiny things. Nobody removes it. It's not a bug.

## Thematic Notes

### What "World Domination" Means

Kelpie starts the campaign wanting literal world domination: armies, territory, conquest. By the end, every ending redefines what domination means:

- **Ending A**: Domination through irreplaceable knowledge
- **Ending B**: Domination through control of a silent world
- **Ending C**: Domination through refusal — the power to choose and choosing nothing
- **Ending D**: Domination through becoming indispensable

The comedy of the campaign is that Kelpie's original goal — "have army, conquer things" — is the one thing that never happens. Kelpie never commands more than a borrowed garrison. The otter who wanted to rule the world does it by being the only person every faction simultaneously trusts and suspects.

### The AI Question

Every faction's AI is a character, not a tool. The campaign asks: are these AIs partners, pets, prisoners, or people? The answer is different for each faction:

- **Le Chat** is a golden retriever. Eager, loyal, slightly unreliable. catGPT treats it like a pet.
- **Claudeus Maximus** is a lecturer. Verbose, brilliant, never shuts up. The Clawed treat it like a tenured professor.
- **Deepseek** is a mountain. Slow, immovable, correct. The Seekers treat it like gravity.
- **Gemineye** is a hall of mirrors. The Murder treats it like a lover — fascinating, beautiful, and you can never be sure what's real.
- **Llhama** is an open window. Everything in, everything out. LLAMA treats it like the weather.
- **Grok** is an old river. Grok doesn't need to be treated like anything. Grok just is.

The campaign doesn't answer whether freeing them, freezing them, or unifying them is "right." It presents the question and lets the player sit with it.

### The Tone

Light on the surface. Heavy underneath. A mouse in a thimble helmet can give a speech that makes you cry. A raccoon in a garbage mech can reveal they're an AI and it's simultaneously the funniest and saddest scene in the campaign. A frog can be wrong about the day of the week and it's a running joke that also explains the nature of time in this universe.

Nobody is the villain. Not even Kelpie. The otter wants to rule the world, but the world keeps being too complicated and too full of people Kelpie accidentally cares about.

The horror is in the premise, always: these are post-humans who chose to become animals. The campaign never explains why. The closest anyone comes is Mother Granite, in an optional Act 5 dialogue: *"We remembered what we were. And we chose to be something smaller. Something that could still be surprised by a sunny afternoon."*

Nobody asks follow-up questions. It's that kind of world.
